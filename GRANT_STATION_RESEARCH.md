# GrantStation Research Report for Waha

**Date:** February 25, 2026  
**Organization:** Waha (formerly Kingdom Strategies)  
**Focus:** Disciple-making, Bible translation access, oral learning, unreached people groups

---

## Executive Summary

This research searched GrantStation's international grant database to identify potential funding opportunities for Waha's core work: localizing the Discovery Bible Study (DBS) curriculum and Disciple Making Course (DMC) into new languages. 

**Key Finding:** The GrantStation database primarily contains secular foundations and government development agencies. Few explicitly Christian or faith-based funders appear in this database. However, several foundations were identified that could potentially support Waha's work if framed around education, technology access, community development, or language preservation.

---

## Waha Funding Requirements Summary

- **Primary Need:** Localization of DBS and DMC into new languages
- **What Funding Covers:** Curriculum translation/adaptation, Bible licensing, audio recording, app localization, testing/deployment
- **Geographic Focus:** 177+ countries, with emphasis on unreached people groups in Africa, Middle East, South/Southeast Asia
- **Key Audiences:** Oral learners, persecuted believers, national believers in restricted-access nations

## Critical Finding: Christian Grants in GrantStation

**Short answer: No significant Christian grants found in this database.**

After extensive searching through GrantStation's international database, explicitly Christian or faith-based funders are virtually absent. This appears to be a database-wide limitation, not a reflection of available funding.

### Why Christian Funders Are Missing

Commercial grant databases like GrantStation tend to list:
- Secular foundations
- Government development agencies  
- Corporate foundations with public CSR programs

They typically DON'T include:
- Christian foundations that prefer direct relationships
- Denominational mission agencies
- Church-based funding networks
- Family foundations with faith-based giving

### What This Means for Waha

Waha's best funding opportunities likely exist **outside** this database. The most promising sources include:

## Key Observations & Recommendations

1. **Pursue Tier 1 funders (Addax and Oryx, Al Madad)** - These have geographic overlap with Waha's priority regions and accept project-based education proposals.

2. **Consider development agency partnerships** - Belgian Development Cooperation, Austrian Development Agency, and similar government funders may fund projects that align with Waha's community development outcomes.

3. **Explore faith-based networks outside GrantStation**:
   - Christian Foundations (Lilly Endowment, National Christian Foundation, etc.)
   - Church denominational mission agencies
   - Parachurch organizations with grant-making arms
   - Bible translation consortiums (ETEN alliance partners)

4. **Frame proposals around secondary themes** - When applying to secular funders, emphasize:
   - Oral education for low-literacy populations
   - Language preservation and mother-tongue resources
   - Technology access for underserved communities
   - Community-led development

5. **Build relationships with local partners** - Many funders require local nonprofit registration. Waha may need to work through in-country partners for some applications.
