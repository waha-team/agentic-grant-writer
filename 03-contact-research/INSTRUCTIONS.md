# Stage 03: Contact Research

> **Your job:** For each shortlisted foundation, identify the right person to contact and any warm paths in.

---

## Before You Start

1. Read `CONTEXT/OUR_ORGANIZATION.md`
2. Read `FUNDER_ALIGNMENT_GUIDE.md`
3. Read `CONTEXT/PAST_GRANTS.md` — check for existing relationships
4. Read the `ALIGNMENT_ANALYSIS.md` inside each project subfolder (focus on "Recommended" foundations)

## What to Do

For each project subfolder found in this directory:

### Step 1: Research Contacts
For each foundation in the "Recommended" tier of ALIGNMENT_ANALYSIS.md:

1. **Find the right person:**
   - Check the foundation's website for staff directory, "about" page, or contact page
   - Look for: Program Officer, Grants Manager, Executive Director (at smaller foundations)
   - Search LinkedIn for people at the foundation
   - If no specific contact is findable, note the general inquiry email/phone

2. **Look for warm paths:**
   - Search LinkedIn for mutual connections between Waha team and foundation staff
   - Check if any Waha board members have connections
   - Look for shared networks (24:14, Missional Tech, DMM networks, Bible translation consortiums)
   - Check if the foundation has hosted events where Waha team members might have attended

3. **Gather contact details:**
   - Name and title
   - Email (if publicly available)
   - LinkedIn profile URL
   - Phone (if publicly available)
   - Best method of initial contact (email, phone, LinkedIn, conference)

### Step 2: Assess Relationship Strategy
For each foundation, recommend one of:

- **DIRECT APPLICATION** — Open application process, no relationship needed first. Just apply.
- **WARM INTRODUCTION** — Someone in Waha's network might know someone at the foundation. Worth asking.
- **COLD OUTREACH** — No application form, but we can send an inquiry letter or LOI. Draft a suggested outreach message.
- **ATTEND EVENT** — Foundation hosts events or webinars. Recommend attending to build relationship first.
- **LONG-TERM CULTIVATION** — Great fit but no clear path in right now. Put on watch list.

### Step 3: Output
Create a file called `CONTACTS.md` inside the project's subfolder:

```
# Contact Research for [Project Name]

*Generated: [DATE]*

---

## [Foundation Name]

**Contact person:** [Name], [Title]
**Email:** [if available]
**LinkedIn:** [URL]
**Phone:** [if available]
**General inquiry:** [foundation contact email/form]

**Relationship strategy:** [DIRECT APPLICATION / WARM INTRODUCTION / COLD OUTREACH / ATTEND EVENT / LONG-TERM CULTIVATION]

**Warm paths identified:** [Any mutual connections or shared networks, or "None found"]

**Recommended first action:** [e.g., "Submit online application by March 15" or "Ask Josh if he knows anyone at 24:14 who has a connection" or "Send introductory email to program officer"]

**Draft outreach message (if applicable):**
> [A brief, professional message introducing Waha and expressing interest in the foundation's grant program. Keep it to 3-4 sentences max for initial contact.]

---

[Repeat for each foundation]
```

### Step 4: Move Forward
After creating CONTACTS.md, move the project subfolder to `04-application-prep/`.

## Decision Points

**Default to momentum.** Keep moving unless:

- A top-scoring foundation turns out to be invitation-only with zero warm paths → Add to HUMAN_TODO.md: "[Foundation] is invitation-only. No warm paths found. Josh: check your network for connections to [foundation name]. If none, skip for now and revisit in 6 months."
- You find a contact who seems like they'd be receptive to a cold outreach → Just draft the message in CONTACTS.md and keep moving. Don't wait for human approval to proceed to the next stage.

## Quality Standards

- Every "Recommended" foundation from ALIGNMENT_ANALYSIS.md must have a CONTACTS.md entry
- Contact information must come from public sources only (websites, LinkedIn, published directories)
- Outreach messages should be warm, brief, and specific to the foundation's interests — not generic
- Never fabricate contact information. If you can't find it, say "Not publicly available — recommend calling main office at [number]"
