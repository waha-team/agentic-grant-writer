## Intro: Terms and Goals

> If this playbook is more than ninety days old, please ask the Playbook Owner to confirm that it is still accurate and up-to-date.

### What Are Grants?

A grant is a sum of money given by an organization, like a foundation, government agency, or corporation, to support a specific project or cause. Unlike loans, grants do not need to be repaid. Grants are usually awarded to nonprofits, educational institutions, and community groups to help them achieve goals that align with the grantor's mission.

### What Are Foundations?

A foundation is an organization that provides funding to support charitable work, research, education, or other social causes. Foundations are typically funded by individuals, families, corporations, or trusts. There are two main types of foundations:

- **Private Foundations** – Funded by an individual, family, or corporation (e.g., The Gates Foundation).
- **Community Foundations** – Funded by multiple donors and focused on supporting local or regional needs.

### What Is Our Goal When Looking for Grants/Foundations?

Our goal is to secure funding that supports our mission and expands our impact. When searching for grants, we aim to:

- Identify grants that align with our mission and project goals.
- Build relationships with foundations to create long-term partnerships.
- Ensure that the application process is efficient and well-documented.
- Track compliance with guidelines.

## What Is the Decision-Making Process?

1. **Initial Screening** – The foundation reviews applications to confirm eligibility.
2. **Evaluation** – Program officers evaluate proposals based on alignment, impact, and feasibility.
3. **Recommendations** – Officers recommend projects to the foundation's board.
4. **Board Review** – The board makes final decisions on which projects to fund.
5. **Award Notification** – Successful applicants are notified, and funding terms are outlined.

---

## What Are the Grant Cycles?

- **Annual** – Applications open once a year.
- **Rolling** – Applications are accepted year-round and reviewed continuously.
- **Multi-Year** – Some foundations fund multi-year projects with periodic renewals.
- **Invitation-Only** – Some foundations only consider applicants by referral or invitation.

## Overlaps in Values to Look For

- **Faith-Based Outreach** – Does the foundation support religious or faith-based work?
- **Community Impact** – Does the foundation value building and strengthening communities?
- **Accessibility** – Does the foundation prioritize making resources available to underserved areas?
- **Education and Empowerment** – Does the foundation support training and leadership development?
- **Global Reach** – Does the foundation have a history of supporting international projects or work in developing regions?

## Networking: How Can We Get in Touch with Decision-Makers?

### Research Contact Information

- Look for contact details on the foundation's website (e.g., staff directory or contact page).
- Search LinkedIn for program officers or board members.
- Use grant databases (like Foundation Directory Online) to identify key contacts.
- If direct contact details aren't listed, call the foundation's main office to ask for the best person to speak with.

### Leverage Existing Relationships

- Reach out to colleagues or professional networks for introductions.
- Check if anyone on your board or within your organization has a connection to the foundation.

### Attend Networking Events

- Participate in foundation-hosted webinars, conferences, or community events.
- Join nonprofit and grant-focused events to meet decision-makers directly.

### Use LinkedIn Thoughtfully

- Follow the foundation and key staff on LinkedIn.
- Engage with their posts by liking, commenting, or sharing relevant content.
