# Church Partnership Council (CPC)

Source: `CONTEXT/Founding Documents.pdf`

## Purpose

The Church Partnership Council (CPC) is a body of five trusted senior leaders from Antioch Movement churches. Each member represents their church's formal partnership with Waha. The CPC provides spiritual covering, relational accountability, and pastoral support to Waha leadership, especially the CEO.

Waha remains a legally autonomous 501(c)(3) Missions Society while operating under Antioch Movement spiritual covering.

## Core Functions

- Cover: spiritual covering and intercession for Waha's CEO, team, and mission.
- Shepherd: pastoral care, encouragement, and relational accountability for the CEO.
- Guard: safeguard against theological drift or moral failure; shares CEO-removal authority with the Governing Board in specific cases.
- Gather: semi-regular check-ins with the CEO (at least annually), plus a full council meeting every two years.

## Church-Level Partnership Requirement

In alignment with IRS regulations for a 501(c)(3) Missions Society (26 CFR 1.6033-2(g)(1)(iv)), Waha maintains formal partnerships with at least five churches. CPC members represent those partnering churches.

## Legal Clarification

- CPC provides spiritual oversight, not legal or fiduciary governance.
- Legal and fiduciary responsibilities remain with Waha's Governing Board.
- Joint governance power: CPC and Governing Board may jointly remove the CEO in confirmed moral failure or theological misalignment by a two-thirds majority of both bodies.

## Term and Membership Expectations

- Five senior Antioch Movement leaders, appointed through mutual discernment between Waha's CEO and Antioch leadership.
- Renewable one-year terms.
- Members commit to prayer, annual participation, relational check-ins, and raising spiritual/doctrinal concerns promptly.
- Member churches may give annually (typical gifts noted in source: $1,000 to $10,000), but giving is not required.
