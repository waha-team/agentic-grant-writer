# Supplemental Tax Information for Ministers

## Important Disclaimer

This guidance is informational and does not constitute personal tax advice.

## Core Tax Status

- Ministers are generally treated as dual status: employee for income tax, self-employed for Social Security/Medicare.
- Ministers receive a W-2.
- Ministers are generally responsible for full self-employment tax (15.3%) on ministerial earnings.

## Taxes Ministers May Owe

1. Federal income tax
2. Self-employment tax (FICA-equivalent)
3. State/local taxes (varies by location)

## Practical Planning Guidance

- Estimate total annual liability (federal + self-employment + state/local).
- Use `Form 1040-ES` for estimated tax planning and quarterly payments.
- If expected balance due is significant, quarterly payments are typically required.

## Withholding Option

Waha may withhold a fixed dollar amount from pay for estimated federal/self-employment tax if requested (e.g., via `W-4` line 4(c) dollar amount).

## Housing Allowance

- Eligible ordained/licensed/commissioned ministers may request housing allowance.
- Allowance is generally excluded from federal income tax but not from self-employment tax.
- Requires advance Governing Board approval.

## Social Security Exemption

Ministers with qualifying conscientious objection may file `Form 4361` (strict timing rules apply). This decision has long-term benefit implications and should be reviewed with a tax professional.

## Recordkeeping

Retain receipts and supporting documentation (housing, business/ministry expenses, etc.) for seven years.

## Frequently Referenced Forms

- Schedule A
- Schedule B
- Schedule EIC
- Schedule SE
- Form 4868
- Form 8812
- Form 1040-ES

## Potential Tax Minimizers Mentioned in Source

- Unreimbursed business/professional expenses
- Housing allowance
- Per diem treatment for qualifying ministry travel meals
- Moving expenses (subject to tax-law limitations)

## Notes on Deason Rule (as referenced in source)

Source material includes Deason Rule examples affecting certain Schedule A treatment where housing allowance is involved. Because this area is technical and fact-dependent, apply only with qualified advisor review.

## Source Caveat

The source includes examples with legacy-year numeric thresholds. Use current IRS limits and current-year rules when applying in practice.
