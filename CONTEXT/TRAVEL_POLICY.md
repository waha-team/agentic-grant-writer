# Travel Policy

## Purpose

Waha provides travel budgets to certain staff for ministry-related purposes such as attending conferences, donor engagement, or regional strategy meetings. This policy outlines the expectations and controls for the appropriate use of those funds.

## Approved Travel-Related Expenses

Staff with approved travel budgets may use their designated Waha corporate card to cover:

- Airfare or train tickets for pre-approved travel
- Conference or event registration fees
- Lodging and accommodations
- Ground transportation (e.g., taxis, rideshares, car rentals)
- Mileage reimbursement (if using a personal vehicle)
- Meals while traveling on behalf of Waha

## Ground Transportation

Staff are expected to use the most economical form of ground transportation appropriate to the context. When possible, this order of preference should be followed:

1. Courtesy vehicles or shuttles
2. Shared shuttles or public transit
3. Rideshares or taxis
4. Rental cars (only when more cost-effective than rideshares)
5. Personal vehicle use (reimbursed at IRS-approved rate; reimbursement cannot exceed coach-class round-trip flight cost when flying would be standard)

Reasonable parking and tolls may be reimbursed. Parking tickets, valet services, and car washes are not reimbursable.

## Corporate Card Usage

- Card assignment: travel-budget staff receive a Waha corporate card tied to specific categories.
- Controls: cards include spending limits and category restrictions (e.g., no cash advances, entertainment, alcohol).
- Pre-approval: all trips and major expenses (airfare, multi-night lodging) require supervisor or CEO approval.
- Receipts: submit within 5 business days via designated expense system.
- No personal charges: accidental personal charges must be reported immediately and reimbursed within 7 days.

## Meals While Traveling

- Use Waha corporate card for ministry-travel meals.
- Waha generally does not provide per diem stipends (exceptions may occur).
- Meal costs should be modest and align with GSA per diem guidance.
- Itemized receipts are required.

## Reimbursements

If out-of-pocket payment is necessary for a pre-approved expense, submit reimbursement with itemized receipts using Waha's standard form within 7 days.

## Non-Reimbursable Expenses

- Alcohol or entertainment
- Valet services, car washes, spa/exercise fees
- First-class upgrades or change fees due to personal error
- Passport fees or personal travel documents
- Expenses for spouses or friends
- Personal gifts or clothing
- Non-ministry conferences/events without prior approval

## Oversight and Accountability

- All card transactions are subject to monthly review by finance or CEO.
- Misuse of funds or policy noncompliance may result in loss of card privileges and/or disciplinary action.
