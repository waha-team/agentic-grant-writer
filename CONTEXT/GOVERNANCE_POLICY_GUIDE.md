# Governance Policy Guide (Direct, Protect, Enable)

Source: `CONTEXT/Founding Documents.pdf`

Status note from source: rough draft, not yet board approved (last edit listed as Apr 17, 2025).

## Board Purpose

Under Scripture and leadership of the Holy Spirit, the Waha Governance Board exists to direct, protect, and enable Waha's mission.

## 1) Direct

The board preserves and safeguards Waha's mission, leadership continuity, values, and culture while allowing methods to evolve.

Highlights:
- Mission focus: multiply disciples and churches among the unchurched and unreached.
- Strategic values include Great Commission focus, obedience-based discipleship, simplicity and reproducibility, global language access, kingdom collaboration, and faithful stewardship.
- Continuity intent: direct policies should remain stable for at least 12 months after leadership transition or major organizational change.

## 2) Protect

The board sets boundaries and monitors compliance to protect mission integrity, user experience, doctrine, finances, and operations.

Highlights:
- App remains free (no ads, no paid barriers).
- Doctrinal integrity is maintained; source also notes Lausanne Covenant alignment.
- Data protection and user safety are emphasized, especially for users in persecuted contexts.
- Funding and partnerships cannot compromise doctrinal foundation or mission.
- Requires documented succession planning and operational risk safeguards.
- Includes ethical AI boundaries (AI assists but does not replace human spiritual discernment).

## 3) Enable

The board supports CEO effectiveness with strategic clarity, prayerful oversight, relational accountability, and resource development while avoiding day-to-day micromanagement.

Highlights:
- Leadership continuity through rotating terms, onboarding/offboarding rhythms, and future board pipeline.
- Board advocacy through ministry, donor, and professional networks.
- Annual policy review and structured calendar for direction, governance, and organizational health.

## Planning Cadence (from source draft)

- Q1: Review Direct policies
- Q2: Review Protect policies
- Q3: Review Enable policies
- Q4: Compensation, benefits, annual budget
