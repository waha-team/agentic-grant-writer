# Search Criteria: What Funders to Target

> AI uses this during Stage 01 to filter foundations.

---

## Target Grant Size — CRITICAL: 990-INFORMED ASK SIZING

- **RULE: Always size the first-time ask based on the foundation's 990 data, NOT our budget needs.**
- Look at their median grant to first-time/new grantees specifically
- If their 990 shows typical grants of $3K-$5K, ask for $3K-$5K even if our project costs $50K
- First-time asks should be at or below the foundation's median grant size
- Build the relationship first. Grow the ask over time with proven results.
- **First-time ask sweet spot:** $1,000 – $5,000
- **Acceptable first-time range:** $500 – $10,000
- **Repeat/relationship range:** Up to $50,000 (only with established relationship)

## Priority Funder Types (in order)

1. **Evangelical / Great Commission foundations** — missions, evangelism, church planting, unreached people groups, Bible translation, disciple-making movements
2. **Broader Christian foundations** — Bible access, global Christianity, Christian education, ministry tech
3. **Catholic / Orthodox foundations** — Scripture engagement, church growth in unreached contexts
4. **Faith-based technology funders** — tech in ministry/missions
5. **Secular development / education funders (stretch)** — Only with strong literacy/community development angle

## Geographic Focus of Funders

- **Primary:** US-based foundations
- **Secondary:** UK, Canada, Australia, South Korea, Germany

## Red Flags (Skip These)

- Explicitly exclude religious organizations
- Require matching funds we can't provide
- Only fund organizations with $1M+ budgets
- Dormant (no grants in 2+ years per 990)
- Application requires existing relationship with zero warm path
