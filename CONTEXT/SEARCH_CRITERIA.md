# Search Criteria: What Funders to Target

> Claude Code uses this file during prospect research (Stage 01) to filter which foundations and grant programs to investigate.

---

## Target Grant Size

- **Sweet spot:** $5,000 – $50,000 per grant
- **Acceptable range:** $1,000 – $100,000
- **Rationale:** Diversifying away from single-large-grant dependency. We want many smaller grants rather than a few large ones.

> **FOR JOSH:** Adjust these ranges based on experience. If you find that $25K+ grants require disproportionate effort, narrow the range.

## Priority Funder Types (in order)

1. **Evangelical / Great Commission foundations** — Most natural alignment. Look for foundations that fund missions, evangelism, church planting, unreached people groups, Bible translation, disciple-making movements.

2. **Broader Christian foundations** — Foundations supporting Bible access, global Christianity, church growth, Christian education, ministry technology.

3. **Catholic / Orthodox foundations** — Particularly those interested in Scripture engagement, church growth in unreached contexts, ecumenical Bible initiatives.

4. **Faith-based technology funders** — Foundations or funds specifically supporting technology in ministry/missions contexts (e.g., connected to Missional Tech, #hack4missions ecosystem).

5. **Secular development / education funders (stretch goal)** — Only if a strong case can be made around literacy, community development, accessibility technology, or education in underserved regions. Frame carefully.

## Geographic Focus of Funders

- **Primary:** US-based foundations (most faith-based foundations are US-based)
- **Secondary:** UK, Canada, Australia, South Korea, Germany (significant missionary-sending nations)
- **Tertiary:** Any other country with active Christian philanthropic foundations

## Values Alignment Signals (from Grant Writing Playbook)

Look for foundations that value:
- **Faith-based outreach** — supports religious or faith-based work
- **Community impact** — building and strengthening communities
- **Accessibility** — making resources available to underserved areas
- **Education and empowerment** — training and leadership development
- **Global reach** — history of supporting international projects or developing regions
- **Technology for good** — using tech to solve social/spiritual problems
- **Bible translation / Scripture access** — the "last mile" narrative resonates here

## Grant Cycle Preferences

- **Rolling applications preferred** — we can apply anytime
- **Annual cycles accepted** — worth the planning
- **Invitation-only noted but not excluded** — flag these for relationship-building in HUMAN_TODO.md

## Red Flags (Skip These)

- Foundations that explicitly exclude religious organizations or religious activity
- Foundations that require matching funds we can't provide
- Government grants with heavy compliance overhead (unless the project clearly fits)
- Foundations with a history of funding only large established organizations ($1M+ budgets required)
- Foundations where the application requires an existing relationship and we have no warm path

## What Makes a Strong Prospect

A foundation scores highly if it has:
- Explicitly funded missions, evangelism, Bible translation, or disciple-making
- Granted to organizations of similar size to Waha ($500K–$5M budget)
- Geographic focus that overlaps with Waha's active regions
- A track record of funding technology or innovation in ministry
- Open application process (not invitation-only)
- Clear guidelines and reasonable application requirements
