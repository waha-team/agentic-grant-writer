# Style Guide for Grant Proposals

> Claude Code uses this file when drafting proposals (Stage 05) and during review (Stage 06).

---

## Voice & Tone

- **Confident but not arrogant.** Waha has real results. State them clearly without overselling.
- **Warm and mission-driven.** We're people who care deeply, not a corporate entity.
- **Story-first where possible.** Lead with a human story, then back it up with data.
- **Clear and accessible.** Assume the reader is smart but not familiar with DMM terminology. Define jargon on first use.
- **Urgent but hopeful.** The need is real (7,000 unreached people groups), but the opportunity is exciting (multiplication is happening).

## Key Terminology

Use these terms consistently:

| Use This | Not This |
|----------|----------|
| Discovery Bible Study (DBS) | Bible study app |
| Disciple Making Course (DMC) | Training program |
| Disciple-making movements | Evangelism campaigns |
| Unreached people groups | Lost people |
| Restricted-access nations | Closed countries |
| Multiplication | Growth / expansion |
| Facilitate (a DBS) | Lead / teach |
| Security-conscious areas | Dangerous places |
| Heart language | Native language (when emphasizing emotional/spiritual connection) |

## What to Emphasize

1. **Simplicity** — "Anyone who can use Spotify can facilitate a Bible study." This is Waha's superpower.
2. **Multiplication** — Not just adding users but producing leaders who produce leaders.
3. **Technology leverage** — Small investment, massive reach. Waha scales in ways traditional missions cannot.
4. **The gap we fill** — Bible translations exist but aren't reaching people. Waha is the "last mile."
5. **Evidence** — Specific numbers, specific stories, specific countries (where safe to name).

## What to Be Careful About

- **Security-sensitive regions:** Never name specific believers, churches, or exact locations in restricted-access nations. Use general regional terms (Middle East, Central Asia, North Africa) unless the project explicitly operates in an open context.
- **Proselytism language:** For Catholic/Orthodox funders, avoid "evangelism" and "conversion" language. Use "Scripture engagement," "community formation," "discipleship."
- **Denominational neutrality:** Waha serves across traditions. Don't position as belonging to any specific denomination unless the funder is explicitly aligned with one.
- **Overpromising:** Don't promise specific conversion numbers or church plants. Promise process outcomes (groups started, leaders trained, languages deployed).

## Structure for Proposals

Most grant proposals follow some version of this structure. Adapt to each funder's requirements:

1. **Executive Summary** — One paragraph: who we are, what we're asking for, why it matters.
2. **The Problem** — What gap exists? Who is underserved? Why does this matter now?
3. **Our Solution** — What Waha does and why it works. Keep it concrete.
4. **Project Description** — What specifically will this grant fund? Timeline, activities, milestones.
5. **Impact & Evidence** — What results have we seen? What will we measure?
6. **Budget** — Clear, justified, realistic.
7. **Organizational Capacity** — Why is Waha the right team to do this?
8. **Sustainability** — How will this work continue after the grant period?

## Formatting

- Use clear headings and short paragraphs
- Include at least one human story per proposal
- Include at least one specific data point per page
- Keep proposals as short as the funder allows — don't pad
- Use active voice
