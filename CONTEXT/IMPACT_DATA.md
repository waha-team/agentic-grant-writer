## Case Studies

> Organized by theme for easy proposal matching. Each links to its published article on waha.app.

### MULTIPLICATION & SCALE

#### 1: Philippines — 30 Groups Become 90 in 3 Months
Pastor "Nathanial" (20+ yrs experience) trained 12 leaders who formed 30 DBS groups of seekers. After a 20-day prayer initiative and 50-day "Days of Faith" campaign, groups tripled to 90 (~500+ people). Members each participate in two groups — one as learner, one as leader — creating a built-in multiplication engine. A woman formerly in sex work said: "I would never have joined this group if there was a pastor involved."
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/how-a-simple-church-in-the-philippines-started-90-discovery-bible-studies-in-just-3-months](https://waha.app/articles/eng/how-a-simple-church-in-the-philippines-started-90-discovery-bible-studies-in-just-3-months)

#### 2: Dreams, TikTok & a MENA Movement — Multi-Country Multiplication
Miriam, a North African migrant worker, had a dream of Jesus telling her to find the Bible. Her roommate invited her to DBS via Waha. Within months, Miriam launched multiple groups that reproduced to 2nd and 3rd generations. She then created TikTok content connecting with Muslims reporting "Man in White" dreams, directing them to download Waha and start their own groups. Over one year, house churches multiplied across multiple countries in the Middle East and North Africa — without Miriam's direct facilitation. When a house church leader was martyred, the movement intensified rather than retreated.
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/dreams-of-jesus--tiktok-leads-to-generational-groups](https://waha.app/articles/eng/dreams-of-jesus--tiktok-leads-to-generational-groups)

#### 3: India — Hindu Priest's Village Embraces Christ
Sunil oversees 1,000+ house churches across rural South Asia. A Hindu priest threatened him with arrest. Instead, Sunil delivered a prophetic word about the priest's son's marriage — which was fulfilled within a week. Sunil performed the wedding, baptized the family, and planted a new house church in the village. The priest responded: "If this God of yours can do that, then I think everyone in this country would follow Him."
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/god-moves-in-an-unexpected-way-to-save-a-hindu-priest](https://waha.app/articles/eng/god-moves-in-an-unexpected-way-to-save-a-hindu-priest)

#### 4: Sweden — 562 DBS in 3 Months Revive a Dying Church
Pastor Johan's Swedish congregation averaged 60+ years old, projecting closure within 15 years. He discovered DMM, contacted Waha to request Swedish translation, then recruited community members to translate, proofread, and record audio. Within 3 months of launch: 562 Discovery Bible Studies facilitated in Sweden. Church transformed "from the verge of decline to incredible multiplication." Johan: "radical confidence that God is not done with Sweden."
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/god-saves-a-dying-church-in-sweden](https://waha.app/articles/eng/god-saves-a-dying-church-in-sweden)

### CROSS-CULTURAL & UNREACHED PEOPLES

#### 5: Hindu Coffee Shop — Healing Sparks Bible Hunger
Arnold, a young Zimbabwean grad student working as a barista in a Hindu-majority nation, watched a DBS group pray for his coworker's sprained wrist. The next day it was completely healed. Hindu and Muslim coworkers began asking spiritual questions. Arnold directed them to the Waha app. His Hindu manager borrowed a Bible and read it every lunch break for weeks. Arnold: "It's not your Bible anymore, man! It's hers now!"
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/what-made-this-hindu-woman-obsessed-with-reading-the-bible](https://waha.app/articles/eng/what-made-this-hindu-woman-obsessed-with-reading-the-bible)

#### 6: Long-Distance Discipleship — Muslim Engineer Finds Christ
Paul (American engineer) maintained weekly video calls with Hamza (Middle Eastern Muslim) using Waha's DBS format. Over a year of study — Hamza reading in Arabic, Paul in English — they worked through Old and New Testament passages. After the Phillip and the Ethiopian study, Hamza declared: "Yes, I think I have [found truth]!" and committed to following Jesus. They continue discipleship via Waha.
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/long-distance-lifechange](https://waha.app/articles/eng/long-distance-lifechange)

#### 7: Malaysia — Blind Muslim Woman Receives Sight at Baptism
Sara, a devout Muslim who prayed five times daily and wore a hijab, was introduced to Bible lessons through Waha. After wrestling one night with core Christian beliefs, she believed. At her river baptism the following morning, as she emerged from the water her physical blindness reversed — she saw shapes for the first time.
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/jesus-opened-the-eyes-of-the-blind-in-more-ways-than-one](https://waha.app/articles/eng/jesus-opened-the-eyes-of-the-blind-in-more-ways-than-one)

#### 8: Iran / Farsi
Partnership with Iran Alive for Farsi translation. Extreme persecution context. Security features critical. Church in Iran growing rapidly.

### PERSECUTION & RESTRICTED-ACCESS NATIONS

#### 9: Militant to Believer — Dreams and an Underground Bible App
In a restricted nation where "one wrong move" means disappearance, a militant group member had recurring dreams of a man in white. He came home to find his roommates reading Scripture on Waha in their native language. They invited him in: "If you read these stories, they might change your life too." He abandoned his militant identity, accepted Christ, and joined an underground faith community using Waha.
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/he-caught-them-reading-the-bible](https://waha.app/articles/eng/he-caught-them-reading-the-bible)

#### 10: Going Undercover — Evangelist Enlists in a War-Zone Army
Malik was arrested for outspoken social media commentary in a Middle Eastern nation. He evangelized fellow inmates in prison. After release, he returned to his war-torn homeland, conducted boardwalk ministry in a coastal city, then enlisted in the military specifically to reach soldiers. He was wounded and presumed dead for months before returning: "I'm still here. The ministry continues." Waha used to help civilians discover Scripture independently.
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/going-undercover-for-jesus](https://waha.app/articles/eng/going-undercover-for-jesus)

#### 11: India — From Persecution to Village-Wide Transformation
Pastor Mahesh's city church tried reaching conservative villages but was rejected: "You come to us with foreign religions. We cannot change our religion any more than we could change the color of our skin!" He pivoted to simple home Bible study groups via Waha, practical service, savings groups, and school-building. Result: nearly the entire village converted, multiple house churches established, a community school built with local funds, and businesses launched. The village priest himself cited Scripture at a mass baptism.
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/from-complete-persecution-to-complete-transformation](https://waha.app/articles/eng/from-complete-persecution-to-complete-transformation)

### VULNERABLE POPULATIONS

#### 12: Witch Doctor to Church Planter — 17 Years of Darkness Broken
Yumna practiced witchcraft for 17 years — curses, rituals, objects placed in dead mouths. Her nephew sent Bible verses for years with no response. When a Waha-equipped church planter visited, Yumna collapsed before anyone spoke: "I know I've been in the wrong... I need the forgiveness of God." She converted, committed to repenting to all former clients, and began sharing Christ. Many churches have since been planted in her village.
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/from-witch-doctor-to-church-planter](https://waha.app/articles/eng/from-witch-doctor-to-church-planter)

#### 13: Refugee Revival — Humanitarian Crisis Becomes a Movement
Miriam leads a disciple-making movement across the Middle East and North Africa. In an Arab Gulf city flooded with refugees who couldn't legally work, she used Waha to share Jesus's stories in native languages. Whole households were baptized within weeks. A police chief's son became a believer and granted free access to a local social club for meetings. Miriam: "If you meet a refugee on the street, there's a high chance they are already in a Waha group reading the Bible."
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/these-refugees-found-so-much-more-than-just-a-job](https://waha.app/articles/eng/these-refugees-found-so-much-more-than-just-a-job)

#### 14: Mauritius Slums — Family Rescued from Poverty and Addiction
Jacque and Marie lived in poverty with constant conflict and Jacque's alcohol abuse. A disciple maker visited and started Waha-based Bible study. During the first session on encouragement, their youngest said: "I'm thankful for you too, Mama!" Week by week: Jacque stopped drinking, conflict ended, children enrolled in school. Marie's sister witnessed the transformation and requested Waha for her own family.
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/god-brings-transformation-to-a-family-in-the-slums](https://waha.app/articles/eng/god-brings-transformation-to-a-family-in-the-slums)

#### 15: From Prostitution to Faith — Home Church Access
Tala avoided churches, believing religious people were "distant, judgmental, and out of reach." A friend brought her to a home gathering using Waha to facilitate Bible reading — making Scripture accessible to someone with no biblical knowledge. Tala returned weekly, began facilitating discussions herself, and was baptized. She later discovered her group connected to an entire network of home churches. Tala: "If I had known a pastor was involved in this, I would have never gone anywhere near it. But I'm thankful I came!"
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/she-never-knew-church-could-be-like-this](https://waha.app/articles/eng/she-never-knew-church-could-be-like-this)

Ben, an experienced American church planter, moved to London but couldn't get new believers to share their faith — they believed "an expert should be the one to do ministry." Before a sabbatical, he implemented Waha's Disciple Making Course. Upon return: multiple discovery groups had started without him, young leaders had developed confidence, and people were being discipled whom Ben had never met.
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/from-one-great-leader-to-many-great-leaders](https://waha.app/articles/eng/from-one-great-leader-to-many-great-leaders)

#### 19: European Church Planting — Team Finds Autonomy
Ben's church planting team in a large European city was dependent on his explicit permission to evangelize. Before a 3-month US sabbatical, he introduced Waha's DMC. Upon return, team members showed dramatically increased passion and initiative. "Obedience is a more regular part of their vocabulary" — they began starting new groups independently.
- **Source:** [waha.app/articles/eng/church-planting-team-in-europe-catches-a-new-fire-for-reaching-their-city](https://waha.app/articles/eng/church-planting-team-in-europe-catches-a-new-fire-for-reaching-their-city)

Church leadership then had the entire congregation complete the Waha course together, now working collectively "to change the trajectory of their community."
