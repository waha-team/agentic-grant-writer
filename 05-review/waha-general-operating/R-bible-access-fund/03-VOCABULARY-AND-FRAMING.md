## Their Stated Values (verbatim from website)

1. "Lift up the problem solvers, like the Close the Bible Gap Alliance, standing in the gap between translation and transformation."
2. "Innovation, righteous risk, and collaboration."
3. "Deliver Scripture in multiple formats — print, digital, audio, video — securely and contextually to the hardest-to-reach places."
4. "Generosity moves with clarity and confidence." (DAF-specific — for donors)
5. "By 2033, every language will have some Scripture translated." → the goal beyond translation is transformation.

---

## How We Map to Each Value

1. **Problem solvers closing the gap** → Waha is a problem solver. We identified that translated Scripture sits unused (the last-mile problem) and built a solution: audio-guided, offline, community-based discovery that doesn't require pastors, training, or connectivity.

2. **Innovation and righteous risk** → Waha's security mode (disguise behind a calculator or piano app) was a righteous risk — building for persecuted believers before it was standard practice. Our SD card / solar player distribution was innovation for communities with no phones. Our 7%-of-verses strategy to launch before full translation is complete is righteous risk in service of urgency.

3. **Audio, digital, video — securely and contextually** → This is Waha's exact spec sheet. Audio: every lesson recorded by native speakers. Digital: iOS, Android, web. Secure: CISSP/CISM certified encryption audit, disguise mode. Contextual: native-speaker localization and cultural adaptation.

4. **Generosity with clarity** → For SWGP donors, Waha offers an extremely clear impact metric: a specific language launch at a specific cost, reaching a specific people group. Dollar-per-language-localized is one of the clearest ROI metrics in the space.

5. **2033 goal** → Waha's 3-year plan to reach 100 languages directly contributes to the 2033 every-language access goal.

## Framing Recommendations
- **Lead with:** "Close the Bible Gap" — use this phrase. It is their language and it is exactly Waha's positioning.
- **Emphasize:** Audio + security + offline — the three things that make Waha uniquely suited to the "hardest-to-reach places."
- **YouVersion connection:** Name-drop the YouVersion partnership immediately — "We partner with YouVersion, and Jenny Tangeman (CSO) publicly endorses Waha." This connects Waha to their foundational relationship.
- **Avoid:** Long organizational history. Be specific and forward-looking. They want problem-solvers, not established institutions.
- **Tone:** Vision-driven, urgent, innovation-focused. Match their language of "righteous risk" and "unprecedented collaboration."
- **Funder type:** Evangelical / Bible access. Full biblical framing is appropriate.

## Specific Grants / Vetted Organizations Relevant to Waha

The SWGP platform vets organizations before listing them. Vetted partners likely include:
- **YouVersion** — the founding story of the SWGP initiative
- **Close the Bible Gap Alliance members** — includes major ETEN/illumiNations partners
- Organizations in the "digital Scripture access" space

Waha needs to be vetted and listed on the platform. This is the primary ask — not a grant from a foundation, but platform listing that makes Waha accessible to SWGP donors.
