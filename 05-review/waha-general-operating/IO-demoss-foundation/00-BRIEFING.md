# Briefing: Arthur S. DeMoss Foundation

> **Status: IO — Invite Only / Operating Foundation**
> The Arthur S. DeMoss Foundation is an operating foundation — it primarily runs its own programs rather than making grants to external organizations. It does NOT respond to unsolicited applications. Any relationship would require personal connection to the DeMoss family (Nancy DeMoss Wolgemuth is the most prominent public figure in the family). Despite the IO status, the foundation's mission language — "technical assistance to missionaries" and "Christian evangelisation and discipleship of the peoples of the world" — is directly aligned with Waha's work.

## History & Context

Arthur S. DeMoss was a successful insurance entrepreneur and evangelical Christian who built A.L. Williams (later Primerica). He died in 1979. The foundation was established in 1959 to perpetuate his evangelical giving commitments.

The most prominent DeMoss family member today is **Nancy DeMoss Wolgemuth** — author, broadcaster, and founder of Revive Our Hearts ministry. She is the chairman of the board and treasurer. Nancy DeMoss Wolgemuth has a significant evangelical media presence focused on women's biblical teaching and discipleship.

Charlotte DeMoss is Secretary/Assistant Treasurer, and Elisabeth DeMoss is Assistant Secretary — suggesting other family members are involved in governance.

The foundation has historically run large-scale evangelism campaigns (e.g., the "Power for Living" book campaign, which distributed millions of books in the 1980s-90s). The operating model has meant the foundation funds projects it directly controls rather than distributing widely to external organizations.

## Why Waha Is a Fit

The DeMoss Foundation's mission language — "technical assistance of missionaries" — is an underused framing that perfectly describes Waha: a technology platform that provides technical assistance (a free app, localized curriculum, security features, offline capability) to missionaries and national believers serving in hard-to-reach places.

Nancy DeMoss Wolgemuth's focus on women's discipleship and biblical teaching overlaps with Waha's Disciple Making Course model. Waha has documented impact with women as disciple-makers (the MENA region TikTok house church example) that could resonate.

## Why They Might Say No

- **Operating foundation:** They don't primarily make grants to external organizations. They run their own programs.
- **Spending down:** Revenue < expenses suggests a foundation that may be winding down its external grantmaking.
- **No unsolicited applications:** Explicit policy — they do not respond to unsolicited proposals.
- **Access:** The DeMoss family is relatively private. Nancy DeMoss Wolgemuth operates through Revive Our Hearts, a separate ministry, and is unlikely to be accessible through cold outreach.
- **Priority mismatch:** Nancy's focus is women's discipleship in Western evangelical contexts; Waha's primary focus is the unreached and Global South — slightly different lanes within the same theological tradition.
