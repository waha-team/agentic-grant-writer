**Key insight:** The path to illumiNations funding is through becoming a recognized technology partner or innovation project within the ETEN ecosystem — not through a grant application. The ETEN Innovation Lab has engaged with audio Scripture, AI-assisted translation, and digital access tools.

## History & Context

ETEN was established in 2010 as an alliance combining major Bible translation implementers (Wycliffe, SIL, United Bible Societies, Pioneer Bible Translators, Biblica) and philanthropic resource partners — collectively covering approximately 90% of all translation work being done globally ([MinistryWatch](https://ministrywatch.com/bible-translation-organizations-form-alliance-to-translate-bibles/)). The goal was to coordinate investment in Bible translation and prevent duplication.

illumiNations was incorporated in 2019 as the grant vehicle for ETEN's philanthropic arm — consolidating donations from individual givers and redirecting them to member translation agencies ([Charity Navigator](https://www.charitynavigator.org/ein/833552306)). The alliance is supported by several of the largest evangelical foundations in the country.

The ETEN Innovation Lab, based at etenlab.org, was established specifically to develop and fund digital tools that accelerate Bible access — audio Bibles, AI translation tools, digital distribution platforms. This is the arm of ETEN most directly relevant to Waha.

In 2024, ETEN hosted its annual "ETEN Connect" gathering where member organizations and technology partners shared progress. The Innovation Lab is actively seeking tools and partnerships that close "the gap between translation and transformation" — language Waha uses directly.

## Why Waha Is a Fit

illumiNations exists to fund the pipeline from "Scripture translated" to "Scripture transforming communities." This is precisely Waha's self-described position as "the last mile of Bible translation." Where Wycliffe and SIL produce translations, Waha delivers those translations to real people through an audio-based, offline-capable disciple-making platform. The alignment is not incidental — it is definitional.

Waha already partners with two core ETEN member organizations: **Wycliffe Bible Translators** (Richard Hoyle has publicly endorsed Waha) and **Biblica** (Alan White, Senior Director of Partnerships, publicly endorses Waha). These relationships create a direct introduction path. A ETEN Innovation Lab partnership — under which Waha's platform could be recognized as an approved "last mile" distribution tool — would open the door to illumiNations funding.

The ETEN Innovation Lab's stated focus on audio Scripture, AI-assisted tools, and digital distribution in restricted-access nations maps precisely to Waha's capabilities. Waha's security mode, offline functionality, and SD-card delivery system are exactly what the Innovation Lab is trying to catalyze.

## Why They Might Say No

- **Scale mismatch:** illumiNations' grants average $3.5M+ each. Waha's full organizational budget is $1.2M. The organization may consider Waha too small to be a standalone grantee.
- **Partnership model:** They fund member organizations, not vendors or technology tools. Waha would need to be positioned as a partner in the translating-to-transformation pipeline, not just a product.
- **Newer nonprofit:** Waha's 501(c)(3) was established in 2025. ETEN partners tend to be established translation agencies with decades of track records.
- **Duplication concerns:** ETEN may have other audio/digital distribution tools in their portfolio. Waha needs to demonstrate differentiation.

The correct path is cultivation and ecosystem integration — not a direct grant request at this stage.
