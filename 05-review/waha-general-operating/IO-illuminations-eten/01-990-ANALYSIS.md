# 990 Analysis: illumiNations (Illuminations Foundation Inc)

*Source: ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer (EIN 83-3552306)*
*990s reviewed: 2022, 2023 (most recent available)*

---

## Giving Overview
- **Total grants since 2020:** $146,956,792 (43 total awards across all years) ([ProPublica 990](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/833552306))
- **2023 grants total:** $45,036,991 ([ProPublica 990](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/833552306))
- **Number of grants in 2023:** 13
- **Average grant size (2023):** ~$3,464,384
- **Grant range:** Highly variable — from several hundred thousand to tens of millions per award
- **Most common grant size bracket:** $1,000,000 – $10,000,000
- **Annual giving trend:** Growing rapidly. Went from early years of a few million to $45M+ in 2023.

*Note: Specific per-grantee amounts for 2023 are not disaggregated in available public sources. The full 990-PF Schedule I would show individual grantees. ProPublica shows aggregate figures. Detailed grantee list would require direct 990 download.*

## First-Time vs. Repeat Grantee Analysis
- **Do they give to new organizations?** No — not in the traditional sense. Grants go to ETEN member organizations who are integrated partners, not applicants.
- **Typical first-time grant amount:** N/A — the "first grant" to a new member is negotiated at partnership entry, not via open application.
- **Do first-time grantees grow over time?** Yes, within the alliance model. Member organizations receive increasing support as alliance funding grows.
- **Core/repeat grantees:** The major ETEN member translation agencies:
  - Wycliffe Bible Translators (Waha partner — Richard Hoyle endorses Waha)
  - United Bible Societies
  - Pioneer Bible Translators
  - The Seed Company
  - SIL International
  - Biblica (Waha partner — Alan White endorses Waha)
  - Deaf Bible Society
  - Forum of Bible Agencies International

## Geographic Patterns
- **Where do their grantees operate?** Globally — with specific focus on regions with remaining "Bible poverty": Sub-Saharan Africa, South and Southeast Asia, the Pacific Islands, and other areas where minority language translations are still underway.
- The 2033 All Access Goals by ETEN are explicitly global — "every language."

## Thematic Patterns
- **What types of work do they actually fund?**
  - Bible translation (primary): ~60% of grants
  - Digital access tools and distribution: growing category
  - Audio Scripture production: confirmed ETEN Innovation Lab priority
  - AI-assisted translation tools: active area of incubation at etenlab.org
  - Training and capacity building for local translation teams
- **Any surprising grants?** The ETEN Innovation Lab model is notable — it incubates technology projects that may not receive traditional 990-listed grants, but instead operate as funded programs within the alliance.

## Similar Organizations Funded
The following grantees resemble Waha in mission (audio Scripture, digital access) and demonstrate that illumiNations funds the type of work Waha does:
- **Faith Comes By Hearing** — audio Bible production and distribution (indirectly relevant through FCBH's ETEN membership / alignment)
- **Deaf Bible Society** — ASL and sign-language Scripture access (aligns with Waha's ASL content)
- **YouVersion partnership** — illumiNations / ETEN has engaged with YouVersion on Bible access, and YouVersion is a Waha partner

## Recommended Ask Amount
- **Direct grant (if invited as ETEN partner):** $100,000 – $500,000 — sized to ETEN Innovation Lab project grants, not to the mega-grants going to full translation agencies.
- **First-stage engagement:** No dollar ask — request ETEN ecosystem recognition and Innovation Lab engagement.
- **Rationale:** Waha should not lead with a dollar amount. The correct sequence is: (1) partner recognition through Wycliffe/Biblica introductions, (2) ETEN Connect participation, (3) Innovation Lab project discussion, (4) formal funding conversation once recognized as a vetted technology partner.
