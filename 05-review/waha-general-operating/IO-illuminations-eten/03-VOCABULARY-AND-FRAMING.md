## Their Stated Values (verbatim from eten.bible and illuminations.bible)

1. "Eradicate Bible poverty in this generation."
2. "Every person on earth has access to Scripture in their heart language by 2033."
3. Every Scripture in audio, digital, and print formats.
4. Every Scripture in the digital public domain.
5. "Closing the gap between translation and transformation."
6. Innovation: "catalyze and cultivate ideas, incubate, experiment, and develop new approaches."

---

## How We Map to Each Value

1. **Eradicate Bible poverty** → Waha delivers translated Scripture in audio format to communities that have the translation on a shelf but no tool to put it into practice. We close the "poverty of engagement," not just translation.

2. **Every person, every heart language, by 2033** → Waha currently operates in 46+ languages with 69 more in the pipeline. Our localization model is designed to scale toward hundreds of languages. We could be a delivery platform for every ETEN-funded translation.

3. **Audio, digital, and print** → Waha is audio-first and digital by design. We are a purpose-built solution for the audio format that ETEN knows is critical for oral learners.

4. **Digital public domain** → Waha uses approximately 7% of Bible verses — a deliberate strategy enabling launch before a full translation is complete. We work within and respect the translation ecosystem's licensing frameworks.

5. **Translation to transformation** → This is Waha's entire value proposition. We take completed translations and put them into practice through community Discovery Bible Study. No other tool in the ecosystem does what Waha does at this specific point in the pipeline.

6. **Innovation and incubation** → Waha's security mode, offline capability, SD card distribution, ASL video lessons, and AI coaching pilot represent genuine technological innovation developed by practitioners, not engineers in isolation.

## Framing Recommendations
- **Lead with:** "The last mile" and "translation to transformation" — this is ETEN's own language and Waha's most precise description.
- **Avoid:** "App" as a first descriptor. Lead with "platform" or "delivery system" to signal infrastructure thinking, not product thinking.
- **Avoid:** Describing Waha as a standalone startup. Position as ecosystem participant — already partnered with Wycliffe and Biblica.
- **Tone:** Mission-aligned, peer-to-peer, ecosystem-thinking. They work with major translation agencies; Waha should present as a mature, complementary partner — not as a small nonprofit seeking a handout.
- **Funder type:** Evangelical / Bible translation aligned — no need to soften the discipleship language. Lead with Scripture access and multiplication.
