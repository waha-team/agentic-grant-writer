## First-Time vs. Repeat Grantee Analysis

**Pioneers USA ($92,000):** Almost certainly a repeat, multi-year relationship. This level suggests sustained commitment to specific missionaries or the organization.

**The Timothy Initiative ($25,000):** The "Achieve Program" designation suggests a specific program within TTI. Likely repeat support for this initiative.

**Other grantees ($10,000-$15,750):** Range suggests mix of first-time and repeat relationships. $10,000 appears to be a common entry-level grant.

**Waha status:** Would be a first-time grantee. The warm path through Ann Baur/Pioneers makes this viable despite no prior relationship.

## Geographic Patterns

- **Primary focus:** Global missions (no geographic restriction)
- **Missionary support model:** COTW funds missionaries sent through agencies (Pioneers, AIM) rather than direct overseas projects
- **No domestic grantmaking:** All mission grants go to international work

---

## Thematic Patterns

### 1. Missionary-Sending Organizations
Primary pattern: COTW supports missionaries through established sending agencies. Pioneers ($92K) and AIM ($15.75K) represent the majority of giving.

### 2. Discipleship Movements
The Timothy Initiative ($25K) grant reveals interest in DBS-based church planting movements. TTI's "Achieve Program" uses Discovery Bible Study methodology — the exact approach Waha delivers digitally.

### 3. Youth Discipleship
Students International ($11.25K) for "LIFT Students" in Dominican Republic indicates openness to youth-focused ministry.

### 4. Trusted Partners
All grantees are established organizations. COTW does not appear to fund small, unknown ministries.
