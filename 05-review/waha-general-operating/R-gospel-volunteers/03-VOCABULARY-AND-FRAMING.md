## Framing for General Operating Support

**The key shift:** The Castilian Spanish proposal asked COTW to fund one project. The general operating ask positions Waha as infrastructure:

> "When you give to Pioneers, you're funding missionaries. When you give to Waha, you're funding the tool those missionaries carry with them."

**Lead with the Pioneers connection.** COTW has given Pioneers $92,000/year for years. Pioneers missionaries use Waha's DBS materials in the field. A gift to Waha multiplies the effectiveness of COTW's existing investment in Pioneers.

**The Timothy Initiative parallel.** COTW gave TTI $25,000 for their "Achieve Program" — a DBS-based church planting initiative. Waha is the digital delivery platform for the exact same Discovery Bible Study methodology. "TTI plants churches using DBS. Waha puts DBS in every pocket, in any language."

## Tone Guidance

- **Warm, relational, community-oriented.** COTW is a camp — they value personal relationships and family-style community. Not corporate language.
- **Story-first.** Open with a specific story of a Pioneers or AIM missionary using Waha in the field.
- **Avoid missions jargon** like "CPM," "DMM," "T4T," or "UUPG." Use COTW's language: "global evangelism," "gospel impact," "develop leaders."
- **Honor their legacy.** 125 years of "vacation with purpose" — they know what faithful, multigenerational ministry looks like. Position Waha as a new chapter in that same story.

## What to Emphasize

1. The Pioneers partnership — missionaries they already fund use Waha daily
2. The Timothy Initiative parallel — same DBS methodology, digital delivery
3. Leader development — every believer equipped, not just trained professionals
4. Scripture engagement in oral cultures — DBS reaches who churches cannot
5. Multiplication — 30 groups become 90; one woman's TikTok outreach sparks a multi-country movement

## What to Avoid

- "Unreached people groups" — they say "global evangelism" and "missions"
- Technical DMM/CPM language
- Heavy technology emphasis — they care about people, not platforms
- Grant language that sounds transactional — they give to relationships

## Draft "Why We Fit" Paragraph (Gen Op Version)

> Waha exists to advance global evangelism by putting Discovery Bible Study tools in the hands of every missionary and believer — in their language, offline, free. The organizations Camp of the Woods already supports — Pioneers USA, Africa Inland Mission, The Timothy Initiative — use Waha's DBS methodology daily to present Biblical Truths and develop Christian leaders among unreached peoples. A gift to Waha's general operations funds the platform, the localization pipeline, and the mobilization training that strengthens faith in communities your missionaries serve. This is not a new direction for COTW's giving — it is an investment in the infrastructure that multiplies the impact of missions you've faithfully supported for decades.
