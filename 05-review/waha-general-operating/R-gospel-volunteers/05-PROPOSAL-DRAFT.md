## FIELD: Describe the development of the ministry project

Waha was founded by cross-cultural missionaries who recognized a gap at the heart of global evangelism: Bible translations existed, missionaries were going, but ordinary believers lacked a simple, reproducible tool for leading others in Scripture. The Discovery Bible Study (DBS) method — already used by Pioneers USA, The Timothy Initiative, Africa Inland Mission, and hundreds of other mission agencies — needed a digital home that worked in any language, offline, and without requiring pastoral training.

Waha launched as that solution. Since then, the app has grown entirely by word of mouth — no paid advertising — from a small tool used in a handful of contexts to a global disciple-making platform active in 177+ countries across 46+ languages. The growth has been documented: 30 DBS groups in the Philippines tripled to 90 in 50 days. A dying Swedish church launched 562 Discovery Bible Studies in 3 months. Missionaries in the Middle East report over 100 churches started through Waha, with multiplication to the 4th and 5th generation.

Waha is now a 501(c)(3) nonprofit with a $1.2 million annual budget, a team of 12 staff and regional coordinators, and a three-pillar model: Equip (build and maintain the app), Empower (train and mobilize disciple-makers), and Everywhere (localize DBS curriculum into new languages).

### Statement of Need

Imagine a Pioneers missionary — someone Camp of the Woods has faithfully supported — arriving in a remote village in Sub-Saharan Africa. She speaks enough of the local language to connect, but not enough to lead a Bible study. She gathers a group of believers. She opens Waha on her phone. She presses play.

The voice from the app begins in the villagers' heart language: an audio-guided Discovery Bible Study, leading the group through a passage of Scripture with discussion questions that require no literacy, no theological training, and no pastor. When the group is ready, one of those villagers — not the missionary — becomes the facilitator of the next group. And then the next.

This is how global evangelism works at scale.

There are 7,000 people groups worldwide who have little to no access to the Gospel. Bible translations sit completed in dozens of languages without a mechanism to put them into practice in community. The training gap is real: 83% of churchgoers have never been equipped to share their faith (Barna Research). The Pioneers missionaries, AIM workers, and Timothy Initiative teams that Camp of the Woods supports are doing extraordinary work — but they need tools that multiply their impact beyond what any individual can personally do.

### Our Solution

Waha provides Discovery Bible Study materials in 46+ languages through a free, audio-first mobile app — no ads, no subscription, no expert required. The model is simple: gather, press play, discuss. Anyone who can use a music app can facilitate a group. The group itself becomes the teacher.

Since launch, Waha has equipped 82,000+ disciple-makers across 177+ countries — every one of them through word of mouth, with near-zero marketing spend. The app works fully offline for rural and infrastructure-poor areas. For believers in restricted-access nations, a security mode disguises the app behind a calculator or piano interface. For communities without smartphones, Waha content is distributed via SD card and solar-powered audio players.

**What this grant funds:**

A $20,000 general operating gift to Waha funds our Mobilization program — the training, coaching, and regional coordination that puts DBS tools in the hands of disciple-makers worldwide. This is the human infrastructure behind the app: the DMC cohorts that train believers to lead groups, the regional coordinators who coach emerging leaders in Asia, Africa, and the Americas, and the partnerships with Pioneers, AIM, YouVersion, and Biblica that multiply Waha's reach.

### Evidence of Impact

In the Philippines, Pastor Nathanial trained 12 leaders who formed 30 DBS groups. After a 50-day campaign, groups tripled to 90. A woman who had been in sex work said: *"I would never have joined this group if there was a pastor involved."* The simplicity of DBS reached her where traditional church could not.

In the Middle East, a single woman's outreach sparked a multi-country house church movement — entirely through Waha-facilitated DBS groups, entirely without a missionary's direct involvement. Over 100 churches have been planted in that region, with documented multiplication to the 4th and 5th generation.

In Sweden, a dying congregation (average age: 60+, projecting closure) requested a Waha Swedish translation, recruited local translators and voice actors, and launched 562 Discovery Bible Studies in 3 months. The pastor wrote: *"Radical confidence that God is not done with Sweden."*
