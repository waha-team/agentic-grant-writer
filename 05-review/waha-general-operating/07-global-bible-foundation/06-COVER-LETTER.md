I am writing on behalf of Waha, a free, audio-first Discovery Bible Study platform serving 82,000+ equipped disciple-makers in 177 countries and 46+ languages. Like Global Bible Foundation, we exist to bridge the gap between God's Word and the communities that need it most. But where your current partners bring Scripture in printed and written form — the Africa Study Bible from Oasis International, the training materials from WordSpread — Waha brings Scripture in heard form, for the estimated 70% of the global population who learn primarily through listening.

We serve the same people your partners serve. We simply reach them differently.

I am reaching out because we believe a partnership with Global Bible Foundation could extend your reach to the world's oral learner communities — believers in Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, the Middle East, and restricted-access nations who cannot access the read Word but can engage the heard Word. Waha has documented this impact in 177+ countries, including movements emerging in contexts where other Bible engagement methods simply cannot operate: communities with no reliable electricity, believers in nations where displaying a Bible openly means arrest, and oral cultures where reading has never been the primary path to knowledge.

A grant of $10,000–$25,000 from Global Bible Foundation would directly fund audio Bible curriculum production in one or two new languages — producing permanent resources available to thousands of oral learners, free, forever. This is the kind of high-leverage Bible engagement investment your mission was built to make.

I have enclosed a full proposal with impact data, budget detail, and testimonials from Waha users and ministry partners. I would welcome a conversation to explore whether a Global Bible Foundation partnership is a fit for the communities you serve and the ministries you support.
