## Their Stated Values (verbatim from website)

1. "Spiritual transformation begins with access to God's Word."
2. "We bridge the funding gap between donors and organizations worldwide needing resources for Bible distribution, engagement, and discipleship."
3. "Whether through grants or direct purchases of Bibles and discipleship materials, we equip ministries on the frontlines of Bible engagement."
4. "94% of your donation will go directly to resource partner organizations worldwide."

---

## How We Map to Each Value

1. **"Spiritual transformation begins with access to God's Word"** → Waha is the access mechanism. We put God's Word into the ears of oral learners who can't read, believers in restricted nations who can't display a Bible openly, and communities whose heart language hasn't been resourced — until now. "We have seen over 100 churches start because of Waha. Many of these churches have multiplied to the 4th and 5th generation!" — Maya, Muslim Background Believer in the Middle East.

2. **"Bridge the funding gap"** → Waha bridges the gap between Bible translation organizations (Wycliffe, Biblica) and the communities those translations are meant to serve. A translated Bible sitting in a database doesn't produce transformation. Waha's audio-first curriculum puts it to work.

3. **"Equip ministries on the frontlines"** → Waha currently serves 82,000+ equipped disciple-makers in 177+ countries, including in restricted-access nations where believers need both Scripture access and security (Waha's disguise mode and encryption are purpose-built for the frontlines).

4. **"94% directly to resource partners"** → This value signals GBF cares about stewardship and low overhead. Waha's lean model is directly aligned: a team of ~12 serves 82,000+ users in 177+ countries. Once a language is deployed, the audio curriculum is available permanently at zero marginal cost. Every dollar produces a permanent resource.

## Framing Recommendations

- **Lead with:** Bible engagement in underserved oral learner communities — their exact language. Show how Waha is "the frontlines" of Bible engagement.
- **Africa connection:** Emphasize Waha's Africa work and growing language pipeline (Amharic deployed, African languages in the 69-language pipeline). This directly connects to their current partners (Oasis International, WordSpread).
- **Avoid:** Tech-heavy language ("platform," "app store," "API"). Frame Waha as a ministry tool, not a technology product.
- **Tone:** Conversational and ministry-focused. GBF is a small, values-driven organization — not a corporate foundation. Write like a fellow Bible engagement ministry, not a grant applicant.
- **Funder type:** Evangelical, Bible engagement — use "Scripture engagement," "God's Word," "discipleship." Lean into their language fully.
