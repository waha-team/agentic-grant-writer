### 2. Statement of Need — The Problem

**Seventy percent of the global population learns primarily through listening.** Not reading. Not studying a text. Listening — to stories, to conversations, to voices they trust. Yet the vast majority of Bible resources available to global believers assume literacy: printed Bibles, written study guides, text-based devotionals, seminary curricula. For the world's oral learners — concentrated in Sub-Saharan Africa, South and Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and rural communities worldwide — these resources are functionally inaccessible.

The gap is not a translation gap. Bible translation organizations have done extraordinary work; Scripture exists in thousands of languages. Waha's partner Biblica, whose translations power many of our curriculum lessons, has put God's Word into hundreds of languages. Your current partner Oasis International is making contextualized Bibles available across Africa.

**The gap is a delivery gap.** There is no simple pathway from "translation completed" to "oral learner community transformed." A printed Bible requires literacy. A study guide requires education. A sermon requires a church building, a pastor, and the cultural permission to attend. For the estimated 1.5 billion oral learners worldwide, and for the 7,000 unreached people groups with little to no access to the Gospel, these barriers are insurmountable through traditional methods.

Waha calls this the "last mile" of Bible engagement: the distance between a completed translation and the community that needs to hear it.

---

### 3. Our Solution — What Waha Does

**The model is radically simple: gather, press play, discuss.**

Waha is a free, audio-first mobile app that guides groups through Scripture in any language, with no pastor, no preparation, and no special equipment required. A small group opens Waha, listens to an audio-guided Bible lesson in their heart language, pauses to discuss, and applies what they've learned. Anyone who can use a music app can facilitate a Waha Discovery Bible Study.

**Why audio changes everything:**
- Oral learners who cannot read can engage Scripture for the first time
- Communities without electricity can access lessons through SD card and solar-powered audio players
- Believers in restricted-access nations can use Waha's security mode (disguised as a calculator or piano app) to study Scripture without detection
- No training, no preparation, and no pastor is required — so multiplication happens naturally

**What this grant funds:**

Partnership support from Global Bible Foundation would directly fund Waha's **Everywhere** program pillar — the work of translating, culturally adapting, and audio-recording our Discovery Bible Study curriculum and Disciple Making Course in new languages. Specifically:

- **Curriculum translation:** Native speakers translate and culturally adapt Waha's Bible study curriculum into a new language
- **Audio production:** Every lesson is recorded in-language, because Waha is audio-first — oral learners hear God's Word in their heart language
- **Bible licensing:** Waha licenses approximately 7% of Bible verses per language — enough to launch before a full Bible translation is complete — from partners like Biblica and Wycliffe
- **Deployment and testing:** New language versions are tested with local users and deployed through the app and SD card distribution

**Partners in this work:** Waha's localization pipeline is strengthened by our partnerships with Wycliffe Bible Translators ("Waha is exactly the kind of Bible engagement tool we want to use on the field" — Richard Hoyle, Wycliffe), Biblica (whose translations serve our curriculum), and YouVersion ("We want to partner with Waha because they are helping those in restricted access nations engage with the Bible" — Jenny Tangeman, Chief Strategy Officer, YouVersion).

### 8. Sustainability

Waha's model is structurally sustainable:

1. **Zero marginal cost per user.** Once a language is deployed, the audio curriculum is available indefinitely — no printing, no shipping, no recurring licensing. Every grant dollar produces a permanent resource.

2. **Multiplication, not addition.** Waha's Disciple Making Course trains believers who train believers. Growth does not require Waha's staff to grow proportionally.

3. **Diversified revenue.** Waha is actively building a portfolio of foundation grants, major donors, and grassroots supporters — reducing dependency on any single source.

4. **Organic growth.** 82,000 users with near-zero marketing spend demonstrates product-market fit that does not depend on paid acquisition. The resource itself drives growth.

A Global Bible Foundation partnership in 2026 would produce audio Bible curriculum available in perpetuity to communities whose language Waha has never served — delivered through an organizational model built to keep serving those communities for decades.
