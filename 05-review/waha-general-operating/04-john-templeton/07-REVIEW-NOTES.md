# Review Notes: John Templeton Foundation

*Reviewed: 2026-03-03*

## Status: MINOR EDITS (blocked on academic partner identification)

The proposal is substantively strong and correctly framed for Templeton's research-oriented grantmaking. The academic framing, research question structure, methodology design, and budget are appropriate. The primary issue is a structural blocker (no named academic partner) rather than a content quality issue. Once the academic partner is identified, this proposal is near-submission-ready.

## Strengths

- **Research framing is correct and consistent.** The proposal does not read as a ministry fundraising pitch — it reads as a research inquiry. The opening question ("How does removing literacy, clergy training, and internet access as requirements for Scripture engagement change the rate, depth, and sustainability of spiritual formation?") is genuinely interesting and would engage a Templeton program officer.

- **Vocabulary mapping is excellent.** "Religious innovation," "human flourishing," "empirical research," "oral cultures," "mixed-methods," "peer-reviewed output," "catalyze cultural change" — all used naturally throughout. The evangelical language (Great Commission, unreached people groups, evangelism) is deliberately absent from the proposal text, which is correct for this funder.

- **Budget is properly sized and detailed.** $200,000 for 24 months is appropriate for Templeton's typical grant range. The budget breakdown is granular and credible — it shows understanding of what research actually costs. The academic partner receives ~37.5% of the budget ($75K), which is appropriate for a PI's time plus graduate student support.

- **Dissemination plan is strong.** Peer-reviewed journal submission, conference presentations, open-access practitioner guide, and public dataset deposit are exactly what Templeton expects. Many nonprofit proposals fail at Templeton because they don't have this.

- **The phenomena documented are genuinely compelling.** The case studies (Philippines 30→90, Middle East 5th generation, Sweden 562 DBS in 3 months, India village transformation) are intellectually interesting as research subjects, not just inspirational testimonials. They are presented as phenomena requiring explanation, which is the right framing for a research proposal.

- **Sir John Templeton reference in cover letter** is well-placed and appropriate. Shows respect for the foundation's heritage without being sycophantic.

- **Templeton World Charity Foundation note** in 00-STATUS-ACTION-NEEDED.md is valuable — TWCF may be a parallel route and should be researched.

## Issues Found

- **CRITICAL: No academic partner named.** This is the primary blocker. Every section of the proposal that references the academic partner shows "[JOSH: Identify and confirm — see 02-KEY-PEOPLE.md]" or similar. Do not submit the OFI until the academic partner is named. A Templeton program officer reading an OFI without a named PI will decline immediately.

- **CRITICAL: OFI deadline unknown.** The proposal notes that the current OFI deadline is not confirmed. Before investing further time in academic partner recruitment, verify the next OFI deadline at https://www.templeton.org/grants/grant-calendar. If the next deadline is 3+ months away, there is adequate time. If it's within 4 weeks, prioritize urgently.

- **MINOR: Academic partner's research track record section is blank.** The "Research Team and Organizational Capacity" section in the full proposal has a placeholder for the academic partner's credentials. This must be completed once the partner is confirmed.

- **MINOR: IRB process not fully addressed.** The methodology mentions IRB approval but doesn't specify who will handle it (the academic institution, as lead, should handle IRB). Make explicit in the full proposal: "IRB approval will be administered through [Institution] under PI [Name]."

- **MINOR: Waha's organizational credibility as a new 501(c)(3) needs pre-emption.** Templeton typically funds established academic institutions and research-credible nonprofits. Waha's newness (501(c)(3) since 2025, no 990 filed yet) may raise credibility questions. Address this in the organizational capacity section: "Waha's technology platform was built over five years prior to formal 501(c)(3) incorporation in 2025, during which time it grew to 82,000+ users in 177 countries — demonstrating operational maturity that predates our formal nonprofit status."

- **MINOR: Consider adding Templeton World Charity Foundation (TWCF) as a separate track.** TWCF funds similar themes with a specific Global South focus and may have a more accessible entry point than the main foundation.

- **MINOR: One placeholder in 06-COVER-LETTER.md** — "[JOSH: If you have a connection to anyone at Templeton...]" — fill or remove before sending.
