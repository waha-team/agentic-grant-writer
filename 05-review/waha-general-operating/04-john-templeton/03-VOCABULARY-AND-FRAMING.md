## How We Map to Each Value

1. **Interdisciplinary and empirical research on religious innovation:** Waha represents a novel religious innovation: the removal of clergy, training, and literacy requirements from Scripture-based disciple-making. This has never been studied empirically at scale. A Waha-funded research project would be the first systematic study of how audio-based, app-facilitated DBS produces disciple-making movements in oral cultures across diverse cultural contexts — genuinely interdisciplinary (missiological, digital anthropological, socio-spiritual).

2. **Catalyze conversations that inspire awe and wonder:** The Waha story — 16 groups to tens of thousands in a few years, entirely organic, across 177 countries — is objectively remarkable. Research that documents and explains this phenomenon creates conversations that will inspire. Academic papers, conference presentations, and media coverage of research findings catalyze exactly the conversations Templeton's mission describes.

3. **Rigorous, evidence-based methodology:** Waha produces quantitative data at scale (app installs, session completions, user-reported group formations, geographic distribution) plus rich qualitative documentation (video testimonials, field reports, partner case studies). A research partner can build a rigorous mixed-methods study on this infrastructure. The research design will include: baseline surveys in target populations, quantitative tracking of engagement metrics, follow-up interviews with group facilitators, and outcome assessment measuring character change indicators (conflict reduction, behavioral change, prosocial action).

4. **How religious and spiritual beliefs and practices affect human flourishing:** Waha's documented outcomes include: alcohol addiction ended (Mauritius), domestic conflict resolved (multiple contexts), a Hindu priest's entire village converting (India), a former militant leaving violent extremism (restricted-access nation). These are human flourishing outcomes. Systematic research would move from anecdote to data: what character change and flourishing indicators are measurable among Waha DBS participants compared to control groups?

5. **Character Virtue Development in the Digital Age:** Waha is, by definition, character formation through digital media — specifically mobile audio. The research question "Does audio-based Scripture discovery via mobile app produce measurable character change in participants?" fits squarely within the "Cultivating Character in the Digital Age" initiative. The CLAL precedent (Jewish community studying AI and digital ethics) shows Templeton funds faith communities studying digital technology's effect on character.

## Framing Recommendations

- **Lead with:** A research question, not a ministry description. First sentence of the OFI should be a compelling research question: "What measurable impact does audio-based Scripture engagement have on disciple-making movement formation, human flourishing, and character development in oral cultures across the Global South?"
- **Frame Waha as:** A research platform with an extraordinary dataset, not a ministry organization seeking operating support.
- **Avoid:** "Evangelism," "Great Commission," "unreached people groups" as primary language. These are missionary frameworks, not research frameworks. Use instead: "religious innovation," "oral cultures," "spiritual formation," "community transformation."
- **Avoid:** "General operating support" — Templeton only funds specific research projects.
- **Avoid:** Framing the grant as funding Waha's operations. Frame it as funding a study that happens to use Waha's infrastructure and deployment network as the research environment.
- **Tone:** Academic and inquiry-driven. Templeton respects intellectual humility. Frame the proposal around what we don't yet know, what we want to learn, and why it matters for human flourishing.
- **Funder type:** Secular/academic (in terms of framing) even though they care about religion. Use research framing, not evangelical framing.

## Draft "Why We Fit" Paragraph (in their language)

Waha represents a significant and understudied religious innovation: an audio-based mobile platform that enables ordinary people — regardless of literacy level, pastoral training, or internet access — to facilitate Scripture discovery groups that empirically demonstrate multiplication across 177 countries and 46+ languages with near-zero marketing spend. The proposed study, "Measuring the Impact of Audio-Based Scripture Engagement on Disciple-Making Movements in Oral Cultures," would produce the first rigorous, interdisciplinary analysis of how this innovation catalyzes cultural change and human flourishing in precisely the populations — oral learners, restricted-access contexts, emerging-nation communities — where traditional religious research has the least data. In partnership with [Academic Institution], Waha will provide deployment infrastructure, quantitative engagement data, and field access while the research team provides methodology, peer-reviewed analysis, and publicly available findings. This is research in the service of wonder: what happens when Scripture, in any language, becomes available to anyone who can press play?
