## Application Details

- **Type:** Two-stage process. Stage 1: Online Funding Inquiry (OFI). Stage 2: Full Proposal (invitation only)
- **System:** Online Funding Inquiry (OFI) — Templeton's proprietary online portal
- **URL:** https://www.templeton.org/grants/apply-for-grant (OFI portal link on this page)
- **Submission method:** Online via OFI portal (unsolicited inquiries accepted)
- **Deadline structure:** Rolling by program area. Most recent confirmed cycle: OFI deadline August 16, 2025 → Invited proposals due January 16, 2026 (or earlier for select proposals)
- **Current cycle:** Check templeton.org/grants/grant-calendar for current OFI deadlines by program area
- **No geographic restriction:** Global applicants welcome

## Stage 1: Online Funding Inquiry (OFI)

The OFI is a brief online form requesting basic information about the proposed project. It is the required first stage and the primary filter for whether a proposal advances.

### Required OFI Information (confirmed from public sources):

1. **Project title** — Should be research-framed, not ministry-framed
2. **Request amount** — Dollar amount requested
3. **Relevant dates** — Project start and end dates
4. **Why the project is important** — Brief compelling statement
5. **Brief description of activities** — What will be done
6. **People and organizations involved** — Principal investigator, academic partner, applicant organization

### OFI Review Process:

Program staff review each OFI to determine if the proposed project represents "a realistic, strategic, and potentially successful opportunity to advance the philanthropic vision of the Foundation." Staff assess fit with funding areas and alignment with donor intentions.

**OFI decisions:** Program staff will either:
a) Decline (most common outcome)
b) Invite a full proposal
c) Request additional information

Timeline from OFI to invitation decision: typically 4-6 weeks after OFI deadline

## Stage 2: Full Proposal (invitation only)

Full proposals are by invitation only. Requirements are extensive. Based on Templeton's known requirements:

### Required Narrative Components:

- [ ] **Executive Summary** — 1-2 pages. Research framing: question, methodology, significance.
- [ ] **Statement of Need** — The research gap; why this study is needed; what is not yet known
- [ ] **Research Questions** — Specific, measurable questions the project will answer
- [ ] **Methodology** — Detailed research design: approach, data collection, analysis
- [ ] **Project Description** — Activities, timeline, milestones over grant period (typically 24-36 months)
- [ ] **Team Description** — PI credentials, academic institution affiliation, Waha team roles
- [ ] **Budget** — Detailed line-item budget in Templeton's format
- [ ] **Budget Narrative** — Justification for each budget line
- [ ] **Dissemination Plan** — How findings will be shared: academic papers, conferences, public media
- [ ] **Evaluation Plan** — How the research team will assess project success
- [ ] **Sustainability** — How research findings and infrastructure will be maintained post-grant

### Required Attachments:

- [ ] **CV/biographies of key personnel** — Academic PI must have established research credentials
- [ ] **501(c)(3) Determination Letter** — IN CONTEXT/501(c)(3) Letter.pdf
- [ ] **Most recent IRS Form 990** — NOT YET FILED (new nonprofit; first 990 pending for 2025 tax year)
- [ ] **Organizational budget** — Current year operating budget — IN CONTEXT/FINANCIALS_SUMMARY.md
- [ ] **Letters of support** — From academic partner, field partners; strong endorsements from Wycliffe, YouVersion, Biblica would be valuable
- [ ] **Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval** (if human subjects research) — Would be required for interviews/surveys; academic partner handles this
