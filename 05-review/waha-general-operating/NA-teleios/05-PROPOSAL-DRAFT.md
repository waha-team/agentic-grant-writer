### About Waha

Waha is a free, audio-based mobile app dedicated to teaching the Bible and explicitly sharing the gospel in every heart language. Since launch, 82,000+ believers across 177+ countries have used Waha to lead Discovery Bible Studies — making Scripture accessible to oral learners, unreached peoples, and anyone who has never had a Bible teacher in their own language.

Our model is built on the direct engagement of Scripture. DBS is not a curriculum that teaches about the Bible — it IS Bible study. Participants hear Scripture read aloud, discuss what the text says, what it means, and what they will do about it. Discovery questions naturally lead to explicit conversations about who Jesus is, what He has done, and how to receive salvation through faith in Him alone.

### Statement of Need

In 35+ countries in Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and the Middle East, hundreds of millions of people have never heard the plan of salvation clearly explained. Bible translations exist in many of their languages — but most believers who want to share the gospel in those communities have no training in how to teach the Bible, no materials in their heart language, and no reproducible method that they can use without a seminary degree or pastoral training.

The result: new believers who want to share their faith but don't know how; communities with Scripture that never becomes discipleship; a Great Commission that stays theoretical for most of the global church.

Discovery Bible Study addresses this directly. It is inductive, reproducible, and explicitly evangelistic. Each session leads participants directly through Scripture — hearing it, discussing it, and applying it. Discovery questions include: "What does this passage say about God? About people? What does it require you to do? Who will you share this with?" The plan of salvation emerges naturally from the text itself, without requiring the facilitator to be an expert.

---

### Methodology (For Teleios Reviewers)

**What DBS is:**
- Inductive Bible study: participants engage the text directly, not a teacher's commentary
- Audio-first format: a native speaker reads the passage aloud, enabling participation by those with limited literacy
- Discussion-based: guided questions draw participants to discover the meaning themselves
- Obedience-oriented: "What will you do this week with what you've learned?" converts study into action
- Reproducible: designed so that a participant in week 3 can begin leading their own group

**What DBS explicitly teaches:**
- The plan of salvation through Jesus Christ — every Foundations track progresses from Creation to Christ to salvation
- Scripture as the authoritative Word of God — no human commentary mediates the text
- Discipleship as every believer's calling — "Go and do likewise" is built into every session

### Evidence of Changed Lives (Cessationist-Safe Stories)

**Long-Distance Discipleship — Muslim Engineer Comes to Faith**
An American engineer maintained weekly video DBS sessions with Hamza, a Muslim man in the Middle East. Using Waha — reading the same passages in their own languages, discussing the same discovery questions — they studied the Old and New Testament over more than a year. After the Philip and the Ethiopian passage, Hamza said: *"Yes, I think I have [found truth]!"* and committed to following Jesus Christ. He continues being discipled through Waha. No miraculous experience. No charismatic phenomenon. Just a man and a friend reading Scripture together, every week, until truth was clear.

**Philippines — Multiplication Through Bible Teaching**
Pastor Nathanial trained 12 local believers to lead Discovery Bible Studies. Within 50 days, 30 groups had multiplied to 90 — reaching approximately 500 people. A woman formerly in sex work joined because she felt excluded from traditional church. She came in, engaged directly with Scripture, and stayed. *"I would never have joined this group if there was a pastor involved."* The method worked because it led people to the Bible itself — not to a teacher's personality.

**London — Leaders Developed Through DMC**
Ben, an American church planter in London, struggled to get believers to share their faith. After implementing Waha's Disciple Making Course, he returned from a 3-month sabbatical to find multiple new DBS groups started without him — leaders discipling people Ben had never met. *"Obedience is a more regular part of their vocabulary."* The DMC created not one leader, but many.
