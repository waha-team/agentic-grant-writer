# 990 Analysis: Vermeer Charitable Foundation

*Source: Grantmakers.io, ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer, CauseIQ, Instrumentl*
*990s reviewed: 2024, 2023, 2022*

---

## Giving Overview

- **Total grants per year:** ~$1.76M (2024), ~$1.15M (2023) ([ProPublica 990](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/421087640))
- **Number of grants per year:** 75 (2024), 60 (2023)
- **Median grant size:** ~$23,500
- **Average grant size:** ~$23,500
- **Grant range:** $100 – $750,000
- **Most common grant size bracket:** $10,000 – $135,000
- **Total assets:** $31.4M – $34M (2024) ([ProPublica 990](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/421087640))

---

## First-Time vs. Repeat Grantee Analysis

- **Do they give to new organizations?** Yes — but only if invited. 12% of applicants funded ([Grantable.co](https://www.grantable.co/search/funders/profile/vermeer-charitable-foundation-us-foundation-421087640)).
- **Typical first-time grant amount:** Unknown
- **Core/repeat grantees:** Pella Christian schools, City of Pella, Food Bank of Iowa, Central College, Dordt University — appear year after year ([ProPublica 990](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/421087640))
- **Process:** Organizations must submit a letter/email briefly describing their request to receive login credentials for the full application portal ([Vermeer Charitable Foundation website](https://charitable.vermeer.com/)). This is de facto invite-only for external organizations.

---

## Geographic Patterns

- **Where do their grantees operate?** Heavily concentrated in Pella, Iowa (Marion County) and surrounding central Iowa communities. Some Midwest expansion (Illinois, Wisconsin). Occasional national Christian education (e.g., Trinity Christian College).
- **States served:** IA (primary), IL, MN, WI, SD (secondary), plus scattered U.S. states for larger Christian education grants.
- **International:** Minimal. The only international-scope Christian grant found is Christian Reformed World Missions ($10,000, historical). No pattern of international evangelical missions funding.

**CRITICAL FOR WAHA:** Foundation's policy explicitly states grants go to "organizations primarily in the Pella area and other communities from which our team members commute" ([Vermeer Charitable Foundation website](https://charitable.vermeer.com/about-us/)). Washington State is not eligible.

## Thematic Patterns

| Category | Est. % of grants |

| Charitable / Social Services (food banks, retirement services) | ~15% |

**Thematic observation:** Despite the biblical principles branding, this foundation operates primarily as a community foundation for Pella, Iowa and Vermeer employees. Faith-based giving is modest and concentrated in the Christian Reformed tradition, not evangelical missions.

## Application Process Details (for reference)

- **Contact:** Tricia Vermeer, charitablefoundation@vermeer.com, (641) 628-3141 ([Vermeer Charitable Foundation website](https://charitable.vermeer.com/))
