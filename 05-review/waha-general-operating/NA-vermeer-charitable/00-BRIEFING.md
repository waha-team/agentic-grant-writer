# Briefing: Vermeer Charitable Foundation

> **Status: NA — Not Applicable**
> The Vermeer Charitable Foundation has two disqualifying characteristics for Waha: (1) it is invite-only / preselected and does not accept unsolicited applications, and (2) its geographic mandate explicitly restricts grants to organizations in "the Pella area and other communities from which Vermeer Corporation team members commute" — a central Iowa / Midwest focus. Waha is a Spokane, WA-based nonprofit serving 177 countries with no operational presence in Iowa. Even if the application process were open, Waha does not meet the geographic eligibility criteria. This foundation should be archived.

*Research date: 2026-03-03*

## History & Context

Vermeer Corporation is a family-owned agricultural and industrial equipment manufacturer based in Pella, Iowa, founded by Gary Vermeer in 1948. The Vermeer Charitable Foundation was established in 1977 as the company's philanthropic arm. The Vermeer and Andringa families (Gary Vermeer's descendants) control the board.

The foundation reflects the Christian Reformed Church tradition of the Dutch-American farming community of central Iowa — strong on education, community institutions, and Reformed Christianity, but not oriented toward evangelical missions, Great Commission outreach, or international ministry.

## Why Waha Is NOT a Fit

1. **Geographic disqualification:** Waha has no Vermeer employees, no operations in Iowa, no Pella-area community connection. The foundation's mandate requires organizations serving Vermeer employee communities. Waha is categorically ineligible.

2. **Invite-only:** The foundation does not accept unsolicited grant applications. Organizations must be invited or known to the family. Waha has no connection to the Vermeer/Andringa family network.

3. **Denominational mismatch:** Vermeer's faith-giving reflects the Christian Reformed Church tradition (Pella has deep Dutch CRC roots). Waha's partners are evangelical/interdenominational (Cru, YWAM, Pioneers, Biblica). While both are Christian, the grant patterns show no history of evangelical missions or Great Commission organizational funding.

4. **International mismatch:** Vermeer's largest non-Pella grant found was $10K to Christian Reformed World Missions — a modest figure compared to their local education giving. International evangelical missions is clearly not a priority.

---

## Why They Might Say No

- Waha is in Washington State, not Iowa.
- No Vermeer employees use Waha.
- No existing relationship with the Vermeer or Andringa families.
- Invitation-only process.
- No precedent for funding evangelical missions organizations at the scale Waha would request.

---

## Conclusion

Archive this foundation. Do not invest further research time. There is no realistic path to a Vermeer grant for Waha based on geographic restriction, invite-only policy, and mission mismatch.
