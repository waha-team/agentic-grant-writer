# Briefing: Green Family Foundation (Hobby Lobby)

> **Status: IO — Invite Only / No Unsolicited Applications**
> The Green Family Foundation (EIN 27-5341883, green-family-foundation.org) does not accept unsolicited donation requests. Staff explicitly state this. Access requires warm introduction through the family's ministry network. The critical warm path is through YouVersion: Mart Green (a Green family member) specifically funded the YouVersion Bible App from its earliest days, and Jenny Tangeman (YouVersion CSO) is a Waha endorser. The YouVersion connection is Waha's strongest path into the Green family ecosystem. The family has donated over $500 million to evangelical causes and routinely makes multi-million-dollar grants to missions organizations.

## History & Context

David Green founded Hobby Lobby in 1972 as a craft store chain ([Wikipedia — David Green](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Green_(entrepreneur))). The Green family has always operated the business as a Christian company — famous for being closed on Sundays and for the Supreme Court case (Burwell v. Hobby Lobby, 2014) establishing religious liberty rights for closely held corporations ([Wikipedia — Burwell v. Hobby Lobby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burwell_v._Hobby_Lobby_Stores,_Inc.)).

The family donates 50%+ of pretax profits to evangelical causes ([Wikipedia — David Green](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Green_(entrepreneur))). Primary funding vehicles:
- Green Family Foundation Inc (27-5341883) — the main private foundation
- Hobby Lobby's direct charitable giving
- Mart Green's specific investments (including YouVersion)

**Mart Green** (son of David Green) specifically:
- Heads Mardel Christian & Educational Supply (sister to Hobby Lobby)
- Has been involved with Museum of the Bible in Washington, D.C.
- Offered support to YouVersion founder Bobby Gruenewald before the platform launched as a website in September 2007 ([Gospel Patrons](https://www.gospelpatrons.org/media/this-book-is-alive/))
- Has invested in "a Bible app for cell phones" (YouVersion) — directly parallel to Waha

The Green family's giving is comprehensively evangelical and Bible-centered. They are among the largest individual evangelical philanthropists in the United States.

## Why Waha Is a Fit

The Green family funded YouVersion — the Bible App with 500M+ installs. Waha's Disciple Making Challenge is available on YouVersion. Jenny Tangeman (YouVersion CSO) publicly endorses Waha with: "We want to partner with Waha because they are helping those in restricted access nations engage with the Bible."

This is not a tangential connection. YouVersion is Waha's most prominent partner. The Green family has a personal commitment to Bible technology, digital Scripture access, and reaching people in restricted-access nations — exactly Waha's work. The "YouVersion of discipleship" framing could resonate with Mart Green specifically.

The Green family's Highlands College grant ($20M) shows willingness to invest in ministry infrastructure and training. Waha's mobilization and training pillar (Disciple Making Course) is analogous.

## Why They Might Say No

- **Scale mismatch:** The Green family writes $20M checks. Waha's $1.2M organizational budget may feel too small to register.
- **No unsolicited applications:** Staff have explicitly stated this policy. Cold contact will be ignored.
- **Politically complex:** The Hobby Lobby brand carries political associations (Supreme Court case) that some in Waha's international partner community might find complicated. Not a disqualifier, but a consideration.
- **YouVersion itself:** The Green family funds YouVersion directly. They may see Waha as a YouVersion competitor or may defer to YouVersion for Bible tech decisions.
- **Established portfolio:** With $500M+ in giving history, the Green family has deep existing relationships with major evangelical organizations. Adding a new, small org requires specific advocacy.
