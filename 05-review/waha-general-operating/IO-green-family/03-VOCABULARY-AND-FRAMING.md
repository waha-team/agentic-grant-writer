## Their Stated Values (from Green family public statements and giving history)

1. "God Owns It All" — resources are God's; they are stewards, not owners.
2. Bible technology is a ministry multiplier — they funded YouVersion as a bet on reach.
3. Scripture in every hand — distribution at scale.
4. Church growth and multiplication — not just individual salvation, but community transformation.
5. Generosity is worship — giving is a spiritual act, not just philanthropy.

---

## How We Map to Each Value

1. **God Owns It All / Stewardship** → Waha is free, no ads, no paywalls. Every dollar given produces a permanent, zero-cost resource for communities that need it. This is maximum stewardship — nothing wasted, nothing monetized.

2. **Bible technology as a ministry multiplier** → Mart Green bet on YouVersion to multiply Bible access at digital scale. Waha is the next multiplication layer — not just getting the Bible into hands, but facilitating community transformation through the Bible. If YouVersion was the right bet in 2007, Waha is the right bet in 2026.

3. **Scripture in every hand** → Waha operates in 46+ languages with 69 more in the pipeline. The target: 100 languages by 2028. This is Scripture access at the scale the Green family cares about.

4. **Church multiplication** → Documented: Philippines (30 groups to 90 in 50 days), Middle East (100+ churches), India (villages transformed). Waha produces not just listeners but disciple-makers who start new groups who start more groups.

5. **Generosity as worship** → Frame Waha's mission as an invitation to participate in what God is doing, not just to write a check. The Green family's theological framework treats giving as partnership in God's work — Waha's results are "a 1000% return on investment" (Antioch endorsement quote).

## Framing Recommendations
- **Lead with:** The YouVersion connection. "The Disciple Making Challenge you helped make possible through YouVersion — we built it." This is the single most compelling opening.
- **Mart Green specifically:** "You funded the Bible app. We're the discipleship activation on top of it."
- **Tone:** Warm, personal, theologically engaged. The Green family responds to ministry language, scripture references, and genuine evangelical alignment — not corporate nonprofit framing.
- **Avoid:** Bureaucratic language. Foundation jargon. Grant application tone. Speak as a ministry, not as a nonprofit.
- **Funder type:** Evangelical / Great Commission. Maximum ministry language. The Green family is not secular in their philanthropy.
