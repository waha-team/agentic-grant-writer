## Executive Summary

Every follower of Jesus has been commissioned to make disciples. But in today's world — where seven thousand unreached people groups remain without access to the Gospel, where the majority of humanity learns primarily by listening rather than reading, and where millions of believers want to share their faith but don't know how — the gap between the Great Commission and its fulfillment has never been more urgent.

Waha is a free, audio-based mobile app and training platform that empowers ordinary believers to lead personal evangelism and discipleship — no seminary training, no preparation required. Through audio-guided Discovery Bible Studies available in 46+ languages across 177+ countries, Waha puts the Biblical claims of Christ directly before seekers, creating real opportunities to receive Christ as their personal savior. The Disciple Making Course trains believers in the foundations of personal evangelism, sending them out to start groups that start more groups — producing multiplication at a scale no single missionary could achieve.

We respectfully request $10,000 in general operating support to fund Waha's media-communication, discipleship, and missionary support work, enabling us to bring the Gospel to developing nations and unreached communities through audio technology that requires no reader, no pastor, and no foreign worker.

## 1. The Problem: The Great Commission Gap

In 1900, roughly one-third of the world's population identified as Christian. Today — 125 years later — the percentage is essentially unchanged. We have more missionaries, more resources, more technology, and more mobility than any generation in history. And the needle has not moved.

The problem is not motivation. Believers want to fulfill the Great Commission. The problem is method.

**The Training Gap:** Only 17% of churchgoers can identify and explain the Great Commission (Barna Research). Disciple-making has been professionalized — delegated to pastors and seminary graduates. The vast majority of believers sit on the sidelines, neither equipped nor empowered to share their faith or lead others in Scripture.

**The Last-Mile Gap:** Organizations like Biblica and Wycliffe Bible Translators have done extraordinary work translating Scripture into hundreds of languages. But translation alone does not produce transformation. Translated Bibles sit unused without a pathway from "translation completed" to "communities changed." Spreading the Gospel of Jesus Christ requires more than text on a page or a file on a server.

**The Access Gap:** Approximately 70% of the world's population are primarily oral learners — they absorb information through listening and discussion, not reading. Billions more live in developing nations with limited infrastructure. Millions live in restricted-access countries where open Christian activity risks imprisonment or death. Traditional discipleship methods — classrooms, Sunday services, printed curricula — cannot reach these populations.

The result: seven thousand unreached people groups — entire communities with little to no access to the Gospel — and a global church growing by addition when the Great Commission demands multiplication.

## 2. Waha's Solution: Personal Evangelism at Global Scale

Waha was built by frontline missionaries and disciple-makers who understood these gaps firsthand and designed a tool to close them.

**The model is radically simple: gather, press play, discuss.**

A small group opens the Waha app. They listen together to an audio-guided Bible lesson — the voice of a native speaker, in their heart language, walking through Scripture passage by passage. They pause to discuss what they heard, respond to reflection questions, and apply what they've learned to their own lives and relationships. No trained leader required. No preparation required. No reading ability required.

Every session presents the Biblical claims of Christ and creates natural opportunities for participants — including seekers encountering Jesus for the first time — to respond in faith and receive Christ as their personal Savior. This is not a peripheral feature; it is the designed purpose of every lesson. Eighty-six percent of Waha's users are seekers, not already-believing Christians. The app reaches people before they are disciples — and walks them toward becoming disciples who make more disciples.

**Three pillars fund the mission:**

**Equip — Product ($312,000 / 26% of budget):** Waha's media-communication technology: the iOS, Android, and web app that delivers audio Scripture and discipleship curriculum globally. Critical design features: fully offline after one download (for communities without internet), disguise mode (calculator or piano interface for believers in restricted-access nations), encryption (reviewed by certified cybersecurity consultant), SD card compatibility (for areas without smartphones), and ASL video lessons for accessibility. This is the distribution platform that carries the Gospel.

**Empower — Mobilization ($456,000 / 32% of budget):** The Disciple Making Course (DMC) — a 9-week training that teaches the biblical foundations of personal evangelism and sends believers out to start their own groups, which start more groups. Regional coordinators in Asia, East Asia, North America, and the Deaf community. Travel for field worker training and partnership development. Marketing to place Waha in the hands of missionaries and national believers.

**Everywhere — Localization ($312,000 / 26% of budget):** Translating and audio-recording Waha's DBS curriculum and DMC into new languages — currently 46 live, 69 more in the pipeline. This is the "publishing and distribution" work that brings the Gospel into developing nations through their heart languages. Every new language launch creates a permanent resource available indefinitely, at zero marginal cost per user.

**Mission Support ($192,000 / 16% of budget):** Bookkeeping, administration, and fundraising — the infrastructure that keeps the ministry running.

## 3. Evidence: Personal Evangelism That Multiplies

Waha's model has been proven across dozens of nations and cultures. These results document what the Foundation's grant would continue to produce:

**In the Philippines:** A church leader trained 12 believers who formed 30 Discovery Bible Study groups of seekers. After 50 days of using Waha, those 30 groups had multiplied to 90 — with 20% of groups now in the third generation. Over 500 people encountering the Biblical claims of Christ. A woman formerly in sex work: *"I would never have joined this group if there was a pastor involved."*

**In the Middle East and North Africa:** A woman named Miriam encountered Jesus through a Waha DBS group, began leading her own groups, and created TikTok content connecting with Muslims reporting dreams of Jesus. Within a year, house churches were multiplying across multiple countries — in the 4th and 5th generation — entirely without Miriam's direct involvement. One network leader: *"We have seen over 100 churches start because of Waha."*

**In India:** Entire villages — initially hostile to Christian outreach — transformed through Waha-facilitated home Bible study groups. The village priest himself cited Scripture at a community-wide baptism.

**In a Restricted-Access Nation:** A man involved with a militant group had recurring dreams of Jesus. He came home to find his roommates reading Scripture on Waha in their native language. He abandoned his militant identity, received Christ, and joined an underground faith community using Waha. Security mode kept the app invisible on his device.

**In Sweden:** A dying congregation (average age 60+, projected to close within 15 years) requested a Swedish translation. Within three months of launch, 562 Discovery Bible Studies had been facilitated through Waha. Pastor Johan: *"Radical confidence that God is not done with Sweden."*

These are not exceptional stories. They represent a documented, reproducible pattern: ordinary believers equipped with Waha produce extraordinary multiplication of personal evangelism and discipleship.

## 8. Sustainability

Waha's model is inherently sustainable because the core cost is translation — and translated content, once produced, is available indefinitely at zero marginal cost per additional user. Every dollar invested in localization produces a permanent evangelical resource available to any believer, seeker, or missionary who downloads the app, forever.

The Disciple Making Course trains believers who train more believers — multiplication that does not depend on Waha's team size or budget. As disciple-making movements spread organically through indigenous communities, the Gospel continues to advance independent of Waha's direct involvement.

Waha is actively building a diversified funding base — grants, major donors, midlevel supporters, and grassroots contributors — to ensure long-term financial health and mission continuity. The Foundation's investment would be part of a stable, growing portfolio rather than a critical single source.
