## First-Time vs. Repeat Grantee Analysis

- **Do they give to new organizations?** Yes — the annual report shows diverse grantees; many appear in specific years only
- **Typical first-time grant amount:** $3,000–$10,000 based on pattern (smaller grants cluster at this level for new grantees)
- **Repeat grantee pattern:** Capstone Legacy Foundation ($125,000 in 2023 — two grants) is the most extreme repeat recipient (Nancy Huston Hansen is its founder and president); The Graystone Society and Jamestown-Yorktown Foundation received multiple grants in the same year
- **Core/repeat grantees:** Capstone Legacy Foundation (Nancy's own organization), local Chester County organizations, The Graystone Society

**Key insight:** The board has discretion to modify grant amounts. A first-time ask at $10,000–$15,000 is appropriate for an international discipleship/missions organization. Biblica's $25,000 is an aspirational comp for a stronger relationship.

## Similar Organizations Funded (Direct Comparables for Waha)

**Most relevant:** Biblica, Inc. — $25,000 for Reach4Life Project. Biblica is a leading Bible translation and publishing organization; Reach4Life delivers Scripture to young people in Africa. This is the strongest single comp: international, technology-enabled Scripture access. Waha partners with Biblica directly.

**Secondary comps:**
- **Heavenworks** ($25,000 total) — international outreach in French-speaking world. International missions, specific language/region focus. Waha's localization work is analogous.
- **Forge Leadership Network** ($20,000 for general operating) — demonstrates willingness to give general operating support to a missions/leadership development org, though this amount is unusually large.
- **Inner-City Movement** ($10,000 for Family Devotions with ActorsBible) — audio-based Bible engagement for families. ActorsBible uses dramatized audio Scripture — conceptually close to Waha's audio-first approach.
- **Philadelphia Gospel Movement** ($17,000) — evangelistic programming at scale.

---

## Recommended Ask Amount

- **First-time ask:** $10,000 — based on the average evangelical grant size ($10,379) and the range for national non-PA organizations. Biblica at $25,000 is the aspirational comparison, but for a first-time applicant, $10,000 is the calibrated entry point.
- **Future relationship ask:** $15,000–$25,000 — once a relationship is established, subsequent grants can grow toward the Biblica/Heavenworks range.
- **Rationale:** The average evangelical grant is $10,379. For a first-time, non-Pennsylvania applicant with international scope but without an established relationship, $10,000 positions Waha as serious but not overreaching. Nancy Hansen's prior grant to Forge Leadership Network at $20,000 for general operating is encouraging — but that relationship may be warmer than Waha's cold approach.

**Note on general operating vs. project:** The foundation "prefers specific projects and programs" over general operating. Frame the $10,000 ask as supporting Waha's Localization (Everywhere) pillar — specifically, the translation and audio production of new language versions — rather than pure unrestricted operating support. This is honest (it is part of the general operating budget) while being more specific.
