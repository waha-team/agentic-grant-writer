## Their Stated Values (verbatim from operating principles) ([Foundation Website](https://www.stacy.cc/operating-principles))

1. **Relational and Collaborative Giving** — "partner with organizations that our Trustees, staff, peers, and partners know well and trust"
2. **High Standards** — organizations must demonstrate "high reporting and accountability standards" aligned with ECFA
3. **Proven & Indigenous Leadership** — "leaders working within their own cultural context are more effective in accomplishing their mission"
4. **Strategic & Leveraged Giving** — "maximize our return on investment" and prefer "matching grant opportunities"
5. **Replicable, Scalable & Viable Models** — organizations must have sustainability plans and "do not rely on any one funding source"
6. **Community Network & Outreach Models** — "local churches and parachurch organizations that strengthen communities and encourage social transformation, while sharing the love of Christ"
7. **Research & Outcome-Based Granting** — answer the question: "How do you know what you are doing is working?"
8. **Project Specific Giving** — "clearly defined goals and objectives"

## How We Map to Each Value

1. **Relational and Collaborative Giving** → Waha operates through partnerships: Wycliffe, Biblica, YouVersion, 24:14, YWAM, Pioneers. We are embedded in the evangelical missions ecosystem — trusted organizations that the Stacy Foundation likely knows well. A warm introduction from any of these partners carries full relational weight.

2. **High Standards** → Waha received 501(c)(3) status in 2025 (EIN: 35-2894107). While our first 990 is not yet filed (pending 2025 tax year), we can provide: board-approved budget, 501(c)(3) determination letter, and financial statements. We are working toward ECFA alignment as our nonprofit infrastructure matures.

3. **Proven & Indigenous Leadership** → Waha's entire model is built on indigenous leadership: any believer, anywhere, in their own language, can facilitate a Discovery Bible Study without external training, missionaries, or pastors. The Disciple Making Course trains local leaders who train more local leaders. In the Philippines, 12 trained leaders grew 30 groups to 90 in 50 days — entirely led by local believers.

4. **Strategic & Leveraged Giving** → Waha's technology leverage is exceptional: a $10,000 grant funds content that is available indefinitely at zero marginal cost. Every grant dollar produces a permanent audio resource usable by unlimited people. "Giving to Waha is like a 1000% return on investment" — Thomas Flippin, Antioch Discipleship School Director.

5. **Replicable, Scalable & Viable Models** → The Waha DBS model is explicitly multiplication-based: trained leaders train leaders who train leaders. Documented 3rd, 4th, and 5th generation groups in multiple contexts. Waha's revenue is diversifying across foundation grants, major donors, and grassroots supporters — not dependent on any single funder.

6. **Community Network & Outreach Models** → Waha strengthens local churches and parachurch organizations. Pastor Johan's dying Swedish church launched 562 Discovery Bible Studies in 3 months. A London church planter returned from sabbatical to find local leaders had started new groups without him. The model builds local church capacity.

7. **Research & Outcome-Based Granting** → Waha tracks: app installs, monthly active users, new users per month, weekly shares, countries reached. We have documented case studies showing specific measurable outcomes (groups multiplied, leaders trained, churches started) across dozens of cultural contexts. "It's remarkable how Waha has gone from 16 groups to several tens of thousands in just a few years." — Alan White, Biblica.

8. **Project Specific Giving** → Frame ask as: "Waha Technology Platform — Scaling Disciple-Making Through Media and Technology." Specific deliverables: support for audio production, app development, and regional mobilization in emerging nations. Clear goals, clear timeline, clear outcomes.

## Framing Recommendations

- **Lead with:** The "Media and Technology" angle — Waha is a technology tool for evangelism, framed in the Stacy Foundation's own category language. Then pivot to "indigenous leadership" outcomes.
- **Avoid:** "General operating support" language. Always frame as a project. Never use "unrestricted grant."
- **Avoid:** Overly academic or missiological jargon (DMM, CPM, Disciple-Making Movements) — use accessible evangelical language: "Bible study groups," "disciple-making," "church planting," "sharing the love of Christ."
- **Tone:** Warm, relational, humble. This is a family foundation with deep personal faith convictions. Match that warmth. Quote Festus Stacy's "greatest business in the world" philosophy to show you've done your homework and share their values.
- **Funder type:** Evangelical — see OUR_ORGANIZATION.md framing guide. Lead with Great Commission, disciple-making, unreached people groups.
- **Story first:** The Philippines story (30 groups to 90 in 50 days) is the best fit for this funder — it shows church planting, indigenous leadership, and multiplication all at once.

Like Alpha USA — a methodology Stacy has supported — Waha asks "gather, press play, discuss," making evangelism the most accessible business in the world for any believer, anywhere.
