### The Opportunity

In a Philippine village, a church worker trained 12 believers who launched 30 Discovery Bible Study groups among their neighbors. Within 50 days, those groups multiplied to 90 — reaching 500 people, most of whom had never set foot in a church. Every group was led by an ordinary local believer. None had formal ministry training. All they needed was a mobile phone, the Waha app, and a willingness to gather, press play, and discuss.

This is what Festus Stacy called "the greatest business in the world." Waha was built to make it possible for anyone.

---

### The Problem: The Last Mile of Evangelism

There are 7,000 unreached people groups in the world — communities with little to no access to the Gospel. The global church has the will to reach them, and Bible translation organizations have produced Scripture in thousands of languages. But a critical gap remains: **most believers don't know how to lead a Bible study, and most translations never reach the people they were made for.**

Three barriers prevent ordinary believers from sharing the love, mercy, and teachings of Jesus Christ:

1. **The confidence gap.** Disciple-making has been professionalized — delegated to pastors and missionaries. The 99% of believers who are laypeople have been told to "go and make disciples" but never shown how.

2. **The access gap.** Billions of people live in oral cultures where reading is not the primary mode of learning. Billions more are in restricted-access nations or areas without reliable internet. Traditional evangelism tools — books, seminars, church programs — cannot reach them.

3. **The last-mile gap.** Translated Bibles sit unused without a simple mechanism for communities to discover Scripture together. The tool must be simple enough that anyone can facilitate it, audio-first for oral learners, and secure enough for persecuted believers.

Waha was built to close all three gaps simultaneously.

---

### The Solution: Media and Technology for Evangelism at Scale

Waha is a free, audio-based mobile app and training platform that engages culture through emerging Christian media — specifically designed to empower any believer, anywhere, to lead Discovery Bible Studies and make disciples. The model is simple: **gather, press play, discuss.** A group of any size opens the Waha app, listens to an audio-guided Scripture lesson, pauses to discuss what they heard, and decides together what to apply.

No pastor required. No preparation required. No special equipment. Anyone who can use a music app can facilitate a Waha group.

**Key capabilities:**
- **Audio-first:** Serves oral learners and low-literacy populations — designed for how the majority of the world actually learns
- **Offline after download:** Works without internet — critical for rural communities, emerging nations, and restricted-access contexts
- **Security mode:** The app disguises itself as a calculator or piano for believers in persecuted regions — reviewed by a certified cybersecurity consultant
- **Available in 46+ languages**, with 69 more in production
- **Disciple Making Course (DMC):** A 9-week audio training that teaches believers why and how to make disciples — producing indigenous leaders who produce more leaders

Waha is free to every user. No ads. No paywalls. No subscriptions. Funded entirely by donors.

**By the numbers:** 82,000+ disciple-makers equipped. 177+ countries with active users. 46+ languages. ~6,200 monthly active users. ~2,000 new users per month. Near-zero marketing spend.
