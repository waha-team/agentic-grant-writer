Dear Brett, Sean, Sharon, and the Stacy Foundation team,

Festus Stacy believed evangelism was "the greatest business in the world." Waha was built to prove him right — by putting that business in the hands of every believer, everywhere, at zero cost.

We are writing to request a **$15,000 grant** from the Stacy Foundation's Media & Technology category to support the Waha Technology Platform: a free, audio-based mobile app that empowers ordinary believers to lead Discovery Bible Studies and make disciples — no pastor, no preparation, no expertise required. The model is simple enough that it works anywhere in the world: gather, press play, discuss. And it is already working.

In the Philippines, 12 trained local believers grew 30 Discovery Bible Study groups to 90 in 50 days — reaching 500 people, most of whom had never attended church. In the Middle East and North Africa, one woman's groups multiplied to second and third generations across multiple countries without her direct involvement — over 100 churches started, many multiplied to the 4th and 5th generation. In Sweden, a dying church launched 562 Bible studies in 3 months. In India, entire villages moved from persecution to mass baptism through Waha-facilitated home groups. All of this with near-zero marketing spend and no paid staff leading the groups — only local, indigenous believers equipped by a mobile app.

This is what the Stacy Foundation's Media & Technology category was designed to support: emerging technology that engages culture and extends the love, mercy, and teachings of Jesus Christ. Waha is that technology. With 82,000+ disciple-makers equipped in 177+ countries across 46+ languages — and 2,000 new users joining every month organically — Waha has demonstrated the replicable, scalable model your operating principles prioritize.

A $15,000 grant will fund audio content production for three new language deployments in emerging nations, app security maintenance, and media infrastructure that ensures content reaches believers in low-bandwidth and restricted-access environments. Every dollar produces a permanent resource: audio content that, once made, is available indefinitely at no additional cost.
