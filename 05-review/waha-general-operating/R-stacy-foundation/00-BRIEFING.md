> **Status: R — Stacy Foundation explicitly requires prior personal relationship with staff or board before applying.**

## History & Context

Founded in 1980 by Festus Stacy (1902–1995) and Helen Miller Stacy (1907–2002). ([Foundation Website](https://www.stacy.cc/about-us)) Festus built his fortune through Penn and APenn oil and manufacturing companies in Butler, Pennsylvania, selling the companies in 1962. ([Foundation Website](https://www.stacy.cc/about-us)) He was a committed evangelical Christian whose giving philosophy held that "evangelism is the greatest business in the world." ([Foundation Website](https://www.stacy.cc/about-us))

A 1966 tragedy — the death of their 19-year-old daughter Irene Helen Stacy in a car accident — deepened Festus's generosity and commitment to faith-based giving. ([Foundation Website](https://www.stacy.cc/about-us))

The foundation is now managed by the Stepelton family (Douglas A. Stepelton as President, Virlee S. Stepelton as Secretary/Treasurer, Brett S. Stepelton and Sean D. Stepelton as Director/VPs). The staff includes Sharon Falls as Grant Manager. ([ProPublica 990, 2024](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/311706311))

The foundation is an ECFA (Evangelical Council for Financial Accountability) aligned organization and requires grantees to share ECFA-standard reporting and accountability practices. ([Foundation Website](https://www.stacy.cc/operating-principles))

## Why Waha Is a Fit

Waha aligns directly with three of the Stacy Foundation's six funding categories simultaneously — a rare convergence. The "Media & Technology" category describes Waha precisely: a mobile app that "engages culture through forms of traditional and emerging Christian media." The "Evangelism/Church Planting" category speaks to Waha's core mission of empowering ordinary believers to make disciples who multiply disciples — with documented results of 100+ churches started in a single region. The "Leadership Development" category maps to Waha's Disciple Making Course (DMC), which trains indigenous leaders who train more leaders, exactly the "indigenous leadership" model the Stacy Foundation prizes in their operating principles.

Their operating principle of "replicable, scalable, and viable models" is Waha's signature. The DMC model produces multiplication, not dependency — trained leaders go on to train their own circles, and Waha's software delivers this at near-zero marginal cost per user. Their operating principle on "research and outcome-based granting" is satisfied by Waha's documented results: 30 groups multiplied to 90 in the Philippines in 50 days; 562 Discovery Bible Studies in Sweden in 3 months; 100+ churches in the Middle East multiplied to 4th and 5th generation.

Their international focus on "emerging nations" maps directly to Waha's strongest user growth regions: Sub-Saharan Africa, the Middle East, South and Southeast Asia. Waha has users in 177+ countries with near-zero marketing spend — the kind of proven organic reach that demonstrates the "return on investment" the Stepelton family explicitly seeks.

## Why They Might Say No

1. **Relationship barrier is explicit and hard.** Their website, FAQ, and operating principles all emphasize relational giving. Without a prior connection, even a perfect-fit proposal may be ignored.
2. **No general operating support.** The foundation explicitly prefers "specific projects rather than general operating budgets." A proposal must be framed as a concrete project with clear deliverables.
3. **April 1 deadline is imminent.** The 4-6 month typical timeline from application to decision means this cycle's deadline has almost passed for a first-time cold applicant. If a warm path is found quickly, the relationship could position Waha for the November 1 cycle instead.
4. **Brand recognition gap.** Their top grantees (Compassion International, Alpha USA, NCF) are well-known organizations. Waha, at $1.2M budget and ~4 years old, is orders of magnitude smaller. The first ask must be modest ($10,000–$25,000) and framed as a project, not an organizational grant.
5. **ECFA alignment question.** Stacy operating principles reference ECFA standards. Waha is a new nonprofit (501(c)(3) received 2025) and has not yet filed a 990 or obtained ECFA membership. This may not be disqualifying but should be addressed transparently.
