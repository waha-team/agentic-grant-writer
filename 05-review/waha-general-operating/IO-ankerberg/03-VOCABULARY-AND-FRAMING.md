## Framing Recommendations

**For media partnership outreach:**
- Lead with: The audio Bible connection — "You've distributed audio Bibles through FCBH. We're the community platform that activates audio Scripture into disciple-making movements."
- Frame Waha as: The next step after hearing. Audio Bibles get Scripture into ears. Waha turns listeners into disciple-makers.
- Apologetics angle: Waha's seeker-friendly Discovery Bible Study format is powerful apologetics — it lets seekers encounter Scripture directly and ask their own questions, which is more effective than lecture-based evangelism.
- Avoid: Grant/funding language. This is a ministry partnership and media opportunity, not a funding request.

**If pursuing a media appearance on The John Ankerberg Show:**
- Topic: "Audio Discipleship and the Last Mile of Bible Translation" — connects Ankerberg's FCBH work to Waha's next step
- Angle: The 7,000 unreached people groups, oral learners, restricted-access nations — these are emotionally compelling topics for Ankerberg's audience
- Outcome: Audience learns about Waha; some become donors

## Draft Outreach Paragraph (media partnership framing)

"John, you've been distributing audio Bibles through Faith Comes By Hearing for nearly a decade — and that work has put Scripture into the ears of millions who couldn't read it. Waha exists at the next step: taking that heard Scripture and helping people discover it together, discuss it, apply it, and multiply what they've learned into new groups. In 46+ languages across 177+ countries, ordinary believers are using Waha to lead audio Discovery Bible Studies with no training and no literacy required. We'd love to explore whether Waha's story — the last mile of Bible translation — would resonate with your audience."
