## Their Stated Values (inferred from grant history and foundation name)

1. Scripture access in formats people can actually use — especially audio (FCBH giving)
2. Evangelical missions at global scale (Cru giving)
3. Building the church as a community of believers ("Living Stones" theology)
4. Quiet, private philanthropy — no public profile

---

## How We Map to Each Value

1. **Audio Scripture access** → Waha is audio-first by design, serving oral learners in 46+ languages. Like FCBH, Waha is committed to getting Scripture into the ears of people who cannot or do not read. Unlike FCBH, Waha adds community interaction and disciple-making to the audio experience.

2. **Evangelical missions at global scale** → Waha operates in 177+ countries, equipping Cru field workers, Pioneers, YWAM teams, and national believers to make disciples. The platform is already integrated into the organizations Living Stones funds.

3. **Building the church** → Waha's model produces multiplying communities — not just individual listeners, but groups that start new groups. In the Philippines, 30 groups became 90 in 50 days. In the Middle East, over 100 multiplying churches. This is "living stones" being built into a spiritual house.

4. **Private philanthropy** → Waha will respect the Powells' preference for quiet giving. No public recognition required; Waha can report impact without naming the foundation if preferred.

## Framing Recommendations
- **Lead with:** The connection to FCBH and Cru — "We're building on the work of organizations you've supported."
- **Theology:** Use 1 Peter 2:5 framing — Waha is helping "living stones" come together in communities of faith that multiply.
- **Avoid:** Tech-heavy language. Don't lead with "app" or "platform." Lead with outcomes — communities formed, disciples multiplied.
- **Tone:** Personal, reverent, ministry-focused. This is a family foundation motivated by faith, not by impact metrics. Lead with mission, support with data.
- **Funder type:** Evangelical / faith-based. Full discipleship language is appropriate.
- **Length:** Keep any unsolicited outreach very brief (one page or less). Respect the private nature of the foundation.
