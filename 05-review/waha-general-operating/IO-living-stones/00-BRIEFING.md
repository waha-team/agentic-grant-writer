## History & Context

The Living Stones Foundation is a small family foundation led by the Powell family (William C Powell as President, Lilja Powell as Secretary, Elizabeth S Powell as Vice-President) ([ProPublica 990](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/521270668)). All officers serve without compensation, consistent with a family-controlled private foundation.

The name "Living Stones" draws from 1 Peter 2:5: "you yourselves like living stones are being built up as a spiritual house" — suggesting a theology of building the church, consistent with discipleship and missions giving.

The foundation's confirmed giving to FCBH and Cru places it squarely in the evangelical missions and Scripture access space. FCBH is one of the most similar organizations to Waha in the broader ecosystem (audio Bibles, oral learners, global reach), so FCBH funding signals strong alignment.

IS THIS GRANT ACTIVE?" — indicating this is an early-stage prospect where active grantmaking has not been confirmed. The domain noted is livingstonesglobal.org.

## Why Waha Is a Fit

Living Stones has funded both FCBH (audio Bible delivery) and Cru (evangelical missions infrastructure). Waha sits at the intersection of both: an audio-first discipleship platform used by Cru-affiliated missionaries (Waha lists Cru as a partner on waha.app). A compelling introduction positions Waha as "the next step after FCBH" — where FCBH gets Scripture into people's ears, Waha facilitates community-based discovery and multiplication.

The foundation's theology (building living stones into a spiritual house) aligns perfectly with Waha's multiplication model — disciples who make disciples, building multiplying communities of faith.

## Why They Might Say No

- **Scale:** With ~$6.6M assets, this is a modest foundation. If all grants go to large organizations like Cru and FCBH, there may be little remaining for a newer, smaller organization.
- **Established relationships:** Family foundations at this size tend to give to organizations they know personally. Waha would need a warm introduction.
- **Active status uncertain:** The CRM note asks "IS THIS GRANT ACTIVE?" — the foundation's current grantmaking status needs to be confirmed before investing in an approach.
- **No public presence:** The absence of a website or public mission statement makes this a harder relationship to cultivate without personal connections.
