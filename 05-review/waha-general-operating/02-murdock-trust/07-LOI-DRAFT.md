## Organizational Description

*(Approximately 200–300 words — concise for LOI stage)*

Waha is a Washington-state-based nonprofit that builds free, audio-based technology for community-based Scripture discovery. Our platform — the Waha Discovery Bible Study app — equips any person to facilitate a Faith Formation group with no preparation, training, or expertise required. The format is radically accessible: gather, press play, discuss.

Founded by frontline cross-cultural practitioners who understood the gap between translated Scripture and communities that could actually use it, Waha designs its tools specifically for contexts where traditional approaches fail: oral learners for whom reading is not the primary mode of learning; communities without reliable internet infrastructure; environments where open faith expression carries risk. Our security features (disguise mode, offline capability, encryption) reflect this design philosophy.

Waha has grown to serve 82,000+ users in 177+ countries across 46+ languages — all through organic, word-of-mouth adoption with near-zero marketing investment. We are a 501(c)(3) public charity based in Spokane, WA, with partnerships spanning Wycliffe Bible Translators, Biblica, YouVersion, Pioneers, Frontiers, and Cru. We are a Meta Language Technology Partner.

Our 2026 organizational budget is $1.2 million. We operate across three program pillars: Equip (product development, $312K), Empower (mobilization and training, $456K), and Everywhere (language localization, $312K). Our 3-year vision: equip 250,000 people to lead Scripture discovery groups, expand to 100 languages, and hire the organizational leadership team needed to sustain this work.

## Project Description

*(Approximately 300–400 words)*

**Project Title:** Head of Marketing — Organizational Capacity Expansion for Faith Formation at Scale

**What we are requesting:** Funding for one new senior staff position — a Head of Marketing — to build Waha's communications and community engagement infrastructure.

**Why this capacity is critical:**

Waha has demonstrated that our approach works. In three months, a dying Swedish congregation facilitated 562 community-based Faith Formation gatherings after discovering Waha. In the Philippines, 30 groups multiplied to 90 in 50 days, reaching over 500 unchurched community members, with each participant simultaneously participating in one group as a learner and another as a facilitator. In London, a church planting team that was entirely dependent on their leader's direction began independently launching new groups when given a simple, accessible tool.

The pattern is consistent: when people have an accessible tool, they act. Our constraint is not the tool — it is the organizational capacity to help more people find and use it.

We have no marketing or communications staff. Every one of our 82,000 users found us through word-of-mouth. This is remarkable — and unsustainable. Growing from 82,000 to 250,000 equipped community members requires intentional, strategic community engagement, not only organic diffusion.

**What the Head of Marketing will do:**

The position will:
1. Build formalized relationships with Pacific Northwest churches, nonprofits, and ministry networks — establishing Waha as a recognized partner for faith communities in WA, OR, ID, MT, and AK
2. Design and execute digital community engagement strategies — turning word-of-mouth foundations into strategic growth
3. Build development communications infrastructure — newsletters, annual reports, donor communications — that enables Waha to diversify revenue and reduce dependency on any single funding source
4. Manage brand and media presence — press, podcast, social, community — to position Waha credibly in the faith-formation and nonprofit technology conversations

The Head of Marketing will be based in the Pacific Northwest and reports to CEO Jeff Peterson.

**Timeline:**
- Month 1–3 (post-Murdock site visit): Recruitment and hiring
- Month 4–6: Onboarding, strategy development
- Month 7–12: Strategy execution, first PNW partnership cohort
- Year 2–3: Full-scale implementation with declining Murdock support as Waha's revenue diversifies
