## Grant Types Available

### 1. Program & Staff (New Staff or Program Expansion)
The most relevant grant type for Waha. Supports new program creation and expansion of existing programs, plus associated staff additions. Grants "may be used to cover start-up costs and/or related staff member additions." Funding follows a **declining three-year structure at 100% / 67% / 33%** rates. ([Foundation Website — Grant Opportunities](https://murdocktrust.org/grant-opportunities))

**Critical requirement:** Staff hires or programs started before a site visit are **ineligible**. ([Foundation Website — Eligibility Guidelines](https://murdocktrust.org/grant-application-process/guidelines-for-grantseekers))

### 2. Capital Grants
Construction, renovation, land purchase. Not relevant for Waha.

### 3. Equipment & Technology Grants
Funds equipment and supporting technology infrastructure. Recipient must cover 50%+ of purchase costs.

### 4. Initiative Grants (separate track)
- Vision & Call Internships: Ministry internship programs for young adults at faith-based organizations
- Undergraduate science research
- Partners in Science

## History & Context

Melvin J. "Jack" Murdock (1917–1971) was co-founder of Tektronix, Inc. (oscilloscope manufacturer, a cornerstone of Oregon's "Silicon Forest"). He was described as "an avid learner, innovator and entrepreneur" driven by integrity and compassion. ([Foundation Website — Jack Murdock](https://murdocktrust.org/jack-murdock-2)) He died without heirs and directed his estate to establish the Trust "to nurture and enrich the educational, cultural, social and spiritual lives of individuals, families and community." ([Foundation Website — History](https://murdocktrust.org/history))

The Trust was established in 1975. Since then it has partnered with over 3,000 nonprofits and distributed more than $1.5 billion in grants, growing from an initial $91 million endowment. ([Foundation Website — About](https://murdocktrust.org/about-the-trust))

**2023 leadership transition:** Romanita Hairston became CEO. New five-sector framework launched. Grants recategorized into new sectors. ([Foundation Website — CEO Update on New Sectors](https://murdocktrust.org/2023/08/an-update-from-our-ceo-details-on-new-sectoral-model))

**Note:** The Trust's EIN is variously listed as 93-6033612 and 23-7456468 in different databases. ProPublica's Nonprofit Explorer uses 23-7456468 (with the 202313199349103306 filing reference). Use the Submittable portal for applications rather than relying on any specific EIN reference.

## Why Waha Is a Fit

Waha is a Washington-state-based nonprofit (Spokane, WA) with an explicitly faith-based mission aligned with Judeo-Christian principles — precisely the profile Murdock's Civic Engagement sector supports. The Trust's founder intended to support the "spiritual lives" of communities; Waha builds the tools that equip believers to live that out.

The strongest fit is the **Program & Staff (New Staff or Program Expansion)** grant type. Waha's 3-year strategic plan calls for hiring a Head of Product and Head of Marketing — two senior roles that would dramatically expand the organization's capacity to serve the Pacific Northwest and beyond. Murdock regularly funds $100,000–$400,000 grants for exactly this purpose: helping nonprofits hire critical staff to move from scrappy startup to sustainable institution.

Additionally, Murdock funds **organizational capacity building** for nonprofits with proven track records. Waha has demonstrated extraordinary impact (82,000+ equipped disciple-makers in 177+ countries) with a small team. A Murdock grant could provide the institutional infrastructure — marketing leadership, product leadership — to scale to 250,000 disciple-makers by 2028.

The "Faith Formation" subcategory under Civic Engagement is a secondary alignment path if the staffing request needs to be further justified through a faith lens.

## Why They Might Say No

1. **Operating history concern:** Waha's separate 501(c)(3) status dates from 2025. Murdock's guidelines exclude organizations with "fewer than three years operating history." This is the single biggest risk. Mitigate by emphasizing the organization's prior operations under Kingdom Strategies and the continuity of the team and work.

2. **Geographic scope mismatch:** Murdock is focused on the Pacific Northwest as a *region*. While Waha is WA-based, its work is global. Program officers may question whether a global digital ministry "serves the Pacific Northwest." Frame: Waha's organizational headquarters, leadership, and team are in the PNW; the grant funds PNW-based organizational capacity (staff hires in Spokane), not overseas programming.

3. **Five-sector fit:** Waha's work doesn't map neatly to Arts, Health, Science, or Education. The best fit is Civic Engagement & Community Services — specifically the "Faith Formation" subcategory. Be explicit about this mapping in the LOI.

4. **Grantmaking is competitive:** $96M across 378 awards averages $254K/grant — but Murdock receives far more applications than they fund. First-time applicants may face longer odds.

5. **Program & Staff requirement:** Murdock requires that staff not be hired before the site visit. Waha should not hire the Head of Marketing or Head of Product until a Murdock grant is confirmed — or they lose eligibility.
