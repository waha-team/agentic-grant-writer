### Organization Overview

Waha is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit based in Spokane, Washington, building free audio-based technology to equip ordinary people to lead Scripture-based discovery conversations in their communities. Our platform — the Waha Discovery Bible Study app — requires no training, no expertise, and no internet connection to use: anyone who can operate a music app can facilitate a group. The model is radically simple: **gather, press play, discuss.**

Founded by cross-cultural disciple makers who saw firsthand the gap between translated Scripture and communities that could actually use it, Waha has grown from a grassroots tool to a platform serving **82,000+ users across 177+ countries** — with near-zero marketing spend. That organic reach is both our greatest strength and our current constraint. We are ready to build the organizational capacity to serve far more.

### Statement of Need: The Capacity Gap

A dying Swedish church — average age 60+, projected to close within 15 years — discovered Waha. Within three months of launching a Swedish-language version, that congregation had facilitated **562 Discovery Bible Studies** across their community. What changed? Not the congregation's passion. Not their theology. Their capacity. They gained a simple tool and, with it, the confidence to act.

That story repeats across Waha's network: communities that lack accessible Faith Formation tools hold enormous untapped potential. The gap is not motivation — it is method and infrastructure.

Waha faces a parallel challenge at the organizational level. We have proven that our approach works — 82,000 equipped community members, 177+ countries, 46+ languages, all through organic growth with near-zero marketing investment. What we lack is the organizational infrastructure to sustain and scale this work responsibly:

- We have **no Head of Marketing.** All of our growth has been word-of-mouth. A strategic communications leader could multiply our reach among Pacific Northwest churches, nonprofit partners, and mission-aligned communities — turning organic growth into intentional community engagement.
- We have a **team of 12** serving 82,000 users across 177 countries. As we target 250,000 equipped community members by 2028, the current team structure cannot scale without breaking.
- We are **a new 501(c)(3)** (status received 2025) with an urgent window: the next three years will determine whether Waha becomes a sustainable institution or remains a promising but fragile startup.

Building organizational capacity now — through one strategic senior hire — is the leverage point that makes everything else possible.

### Our Solution: Faith Formation Infrastructure for the Pacific Northwest and Beyond

**What Waha does:**

Waha builds and distributes a free, audio-first mobile platform that makes Scripture-centered discovery available to anyone:
- **Audio-first design** — serves oral learners, low-literacy populations, and communities where reading is not the primary mode of learning
- **Offline capability** — after initial download, fully functional without internet
- **Security mode** — the app disguises itself as a calculator or piano for communities in restricted or hostile environments
- **Free forever** — no ads, no subscriptions, no paywalls

The core format is disarmingly simple: a group opens the app, listens to an audio-guided Scripture lesson, pauses to discuss, and applies what they've learned. No facilitator training required. This simplicity is not an accident — it is the design principle that makes multiplication possible. Users become facilitators who invite friends who become facilitators.

**What this grant funds:**

This request supports a **Program & Staff expansion**: the hiring of Waha's first **Head of Marketing**, a senior role that will:

1. **Build Waha's presence in the Pacific Northwest faith-formation ecosystem** — developing partnerships with Washington, Oregon, Idaho, and Montana churches, nonprofits, and ministry networks that could deploy Waha in their communities
2. **Design and execute digital community engagement strategies** — reaching the estimated 85% of Waha's growth audience that can be connected through intentional digital outreach rather than pure word-of-mouth
3. **Build the development and communications infrastructure** — establishing the organizational communications capacity that a $1.2M nonprofit needs to sustain multi-funder relationships
4. **Support the 3-year scaling plan** — contributing to growth from 82,000 to 250,000 equipped community members by 2028

The Head of Marketing will be based in the Pacific Northwest and will focus Waha's community engagement strategy on the region where we are incorporated and where our leadership team resides.

### Sustainability

The 3-year declining structure of a Murdock grant is an excellent fit for this hire because it matches our fundraising growth trajectory:

- **Year 1 (100% Murdock):** The Head of Marketing is hired. Their first major deliverable: build the development communications strategy that diversifies Waha's revenue base.
- **Year 2 (67% Murdock):** By now, the Head of Marketing's work has expanded Waha's donor base and partnership network. Waha's grant revenue has grown from $400,000 (2026) to $600,000+ (2027). Waha covers 33% of the position cost from expanded revenue.
- **Year 3 (33% Murdock):** Full integration into organizational budget. The Head of Marketing's work has established the revenue infrastructure that sustains the position indefinitely.

Beyond Year 3, the position is sustained through the diversified funding base the Head of Marketing was hired to build — a self-reinforcing investment.

Additionally: Waha's technology model is inherently sustainable. Once a language is deployed, it costs nothing to maintain access for users. Every investment in organizational capacity produces permanent, scalable impact.
