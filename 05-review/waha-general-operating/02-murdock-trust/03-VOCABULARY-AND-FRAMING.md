## How We Map to Each Value

1. **Stewardship** → Waha operates at extraordinary efficiency: a team of ~12 serving 82,000+ users in 177+ countries. Technology scales at near-zero marginal cost. Every grant dollar produces a permanent digital resource available indefinitely. We are responsible stewards of every donor dollar.

2. **Relational** → Waha is built by frontline field workers who know their communities. The app's discovery format is fundamentally relational — gather, press play, discuss. Our regional coordinators build personal relationships with disciple-makers on the ground. A Murdock grant enables us to hire staff who will deepen relational ties across the Pacific Northwest ministry ecosystem.

3. **Innovation** → Waha is an innovative solution to a problem the church has struggled with for decades: how to equip ordinary believers to make disciples at scale. "Anyone who can use Spotify can facilitate a Bible study." Audio-first design, security mode for restricted-access nations, SD card deployment for communities without internet — Waha represents exactly the kind of creative solution Murdock values.

4. **Data-Informed** → Waha tracks installs (82,000+), MAU (~6,200), countries (177+), languages (46+), and weekly shares (~500). We are building more sophisticated retention and multiplication metrics. All grant reports will include quantitative outcomes tied to specific milestones.

5. **Humility** → Waha does not build a ministry empire. We build a tool that empowers others to lead. Our model is explicitly anti-dependency: users become facilitators who train other facilitators. Waha staff describe themselves as "platform builders," not missionaries. This is humble organizational culture.

## Framing Recommendations

- **Lead with:** Waha as a Pacific Northwest nonprofit building organizational capacity — specifically, hiring the strategic leadership needed to grow from 82,000 to 250,000 people equipped to serve their communities through faith-based group facilitation.

- **Primary sector framing:** Civic Engagement & Community Services → "Faith Formation" subcategory: "human flourishing through the love of God and love of neighbor." Waha's discovery-based model produces personal and communal transformation — the definition of Faith Formation.

- **Secondary framing available:** Education & Leadership Development (leadership development of disciple-makers) or Nonprofit Leadership & Development (capacity building for the organization).

- **Avoid:** "Evangelism," "church planting," "Great Commission" (too insider-evangelical). Use: "Faith Formation," "Scripture engagement," "community-based discovery," "human flourishing."

- **Avoid:** Framing Waha as a "global missions organization" (Murdock is a PNW funder). Frame: "Washington-state-based nonprofit expanding organizational capacity to serve communities locally and beyond."

- **Tone:** Professional, data-driven, values-informed. Murdock is a sophisticated institutional funder. Proposals should read like well-researched nonprofit capacity-building requests, not missionary fundraising letters.

- **Story use:** Use one story to open — choose a story that demonstrates community transformation and human flourishing, not conversion. The Sweden story (dying church transformed through Waha) or the India village transformation story both show community-level impact.

- **Funder type:** Broader Christian / Judeo-Christian — not explicitly evangelical. Avoid evangelical jargon; use "faith community," "Scripture discovery," "Faith Formation."

## Specific Grants They've Made That Relate to Us

1. **Lighthouse Mission Ministries** — $1,000,000 for homeless services facility (2023, Bellingham, WA). Christian ministry, Washington State, faith-based civic work. Shows Murdock makes large grants to explicitly Christian organizations in WA.

2. **International Deaf Education Association** — $244,000 for Regional Fundraising Staff (2023, Montana). Faith-based international education org hiring staff to grow reach. Very similar profile: faith mission + international scope + staff expansion grant.

3. **A Family for Every Orphan** — $111,000 for New Communications Staff (2024, Washington). WA-based org with international mission (global orphan care) hiring communications staff. Almost identical profile to what Waha is requesting for Head of Marketing.

4. **Abuse Recovery Ministry and Services** — $188,000 for New Marketing Director (2022, Oregon). Faith-adjacent nonprofit hiring marketing leadership. Shows Murdock funds marketing director positions for faith-aligned orgs.

5. **Sanctuary Mental Health Ministries** — $243,000 for U.S. Branch Launch (2023, British Columbia). Faith-based org expanding organizational footprint into new geography. Demonstrates Murdock funds faith-based organizational expansion.

## Draft "Why We Fit" Paragraph (in their language)

Waha is a Washington-based nonprofit working at the intersection of Faith Formation, technology innovation, and community development — three areas central to the Murdock Trust's vision of human flourishing in the Pacific Northwest and beyond. Our audio-based Discovery Bible Study platform equips ordinary community members to facilitate Scripture-centered conversations in their neighborhoods, homes, and workplaces — no prior training required. Like Jack Murdock's commitment to nurturing the spiritual lives of individuals and communities, Waha exists to make the riches of Scripture discovery accessible to everyone, regardless of literacy, language, or location. This grant request — for a strategic Program & Staff expansion — would enable Waha to build the organizational capacity needed to sustain and scale this work, consistent with the Trust's model of building capacity in nonprofits driving innovative and sustainable outcomes.
