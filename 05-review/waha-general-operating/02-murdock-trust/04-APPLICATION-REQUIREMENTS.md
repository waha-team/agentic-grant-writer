## Step 1: Eligibility Quiz

Before submitting an LOI, complete the online eligibility quiz at murdocktrust.org/grant-application-process/. The quiz verifies:

- [ ] Organization is a 501(c)(3) public charity with current IRS determination letter
- [ ] Organization is based in AK, ID, MT, OR, or WA (or project has 50%+ regional impact)
- [ ] Organization is NOT a church/place of worship without separate 501(c)(3) status
- [ ] Organization is NOT a private foundation
- [ ] Organization is NOT fiscally sponsored
- [ ] Organization has operated for 3+ years
- [ ] Request fits one of the five sectors

**CRITICAL RISK ITEM:** The "3+ years operating history" requirement. Waha received separate 501(c)(3) status in 2025. Strategy: Emphasize organizational continuity from prior operations (Kingdom Strategies). If the eligibility quiz disqualifies Waha, contact info@murdocktrust.org to discuss.

## Step 2: Letter of Inquiry (LOI)

Submitted via Submittable portal. Murdock's LOI is a structured online form, not a freeform letter. Based on research, the LOI typically includes:

**Required LOI components (based on Murdock's standard process):**

- [ ] Organization legal name and EIN (35-2894107)
- [ ] Year established and 501(c)(3) status date
- [ ] Organization's primary sector and sub-area
- [ ] Brief organizational description (typically 150–300 words)
- [ ] Project/program description: what you're requesting funding for (typically 300–500 words)
- [ ] Grant type requested (Program & Staff, Capital, Equipment & Technology)
- [ ] Staff position title(s) and description of role(s) if Program & Staff grant
- [ ] Total project cost and amount requested
- [ ] Start date (IMPORTANT: Staff cannot be hired before site visit)
- [ ] Other funding sources for this project

**Key note:** Murdock has specific requirements for Program & Staff grants that must be addressed in the LOI:
- Identify the specific staff position(s) to be funded
- Demonstrate organizational need for this capacity
- Show match funding (Murdock generally wants to see other funders contributing)
- Confirm staff will not be hired before site visit

### Foundation-Specific Questions (anticipated — verify against actual portal)

Based on Murdock's Program & Staff grant requirements:

1. Describe the specific staff position(s) for which you are requesting funding. What qualifications will you seek in candidates?

2. Why is adding this staff capacity critical to your organization's mission at this time?

3. How have you determined the compensation for this position? Is it consistent with your organization's salary structure?

4. Describe your plan for sustaining this position after the Murdock grant period ends. (3-year declining structure requires sustainability plan for Year 4+)

5. What other funders are supporting or have been approached for this project?

6. How does this position advance your organization's long-term strategic goals?

7. Describe how you will measure the success of this staff addition. What outcomes will be different in 3 years because of this hire?

8. Confirm that the staff member will not be hired before the Trust's site visit.
