Dear Rudy, Jeremy, Jennifer, and the Civic Engagement review team,

Melvin Murdock left a mandate that resonates with our work every day: to nurture the spiritual lives of individuals, families, and communities. Waha exists for exactly that purpose — and we are writing to request your partnership in building the organizational capacity to do it more effectively.

We are Waha, a Spokane, Washington-based nonprofit. We build a free, audio-based mobile platform that makes Scripture discovery accessible to anyone — no training, no expertise, no internet connection required. The format is simple: gather a group, press play, discuss. Anyone who can operate a music app can facilitate a community-based Faith Formation group. We have deployed this tool to 82,000 users across 177 countries, with near-zero marketing spend, through word-of-mouth alone.

The growth is extraordinary. The constraint is organizational. A team of 12 cannot sustainably support 82,000 users, expand to 100 languages, and build the institutional relationships needed to grow. We need one strategic hire: a **Head of Marketing** to build Waha's communications infrastructure and deepen our presence in the Pacific Northwest faith-formation ecosystem.

We are requesting **$150,000** — Year 1 of a three-year Program & Staff grant — to fund this position in alignment with Murdock's declining-support structure. The Head of Marketing will focus on:
- Building formalized partnerships with Pacific Northwest churches, nonprofits, and ministry networks
- Developing the communications infrastructure that enables Waha to grow from 82,000 to 250,000 equipped community members by 2028
- Establishing the development communications capacity that sustains organizational revenue diversification

Murdock has demonstrated a pattern of investing in exactly this type of organizational capacity for faith-aligned nonprofits in Washington and across the Pacific Northwest — from A Family for Every Orphan's new Communications Staff to Lighthouse Mission Ministries' expanded services. We believe Waha is a strong fit for this same investment: a Washington-based organization with proven impact, ready to build the institutional capacity to scale.

We welcome the opportunity to share more about our work, our team, and our vision. We are happy to schedule a call with your program team at your convenience.
