---

## How We Map to Each Value

1. **Christ-centered** → Waha's work is explicitly about making disciples of Jesus Christ. The name "Christ Is Our Salvation" and Waha's mission to "multiply disciples among the unreached and unchurched" share the same center.

2. **Christian education** → Waha's Disciple Making Course is a 9-week training that educates believers in the biblical foundations of disciple-making. It is community-based education at a grassroots level — complementary to formal Christian higher education.

3. **Christian arts and worship** → Each Waha language localization involves audio recording by native speakers — a form of worship arts in the heart language. Oral cultures worship through music and spoken word; Waha delivers Scripture in that modality.

4. **Early-stage organizations** → Waha's 501(c)(3) was established in 2025. This is genuinely a start-up phase. CIOS's noted interest in "start-up operations and program development" could align if Waha is framed as building evangelical infrastructure for global disciple-making.

## Framing Recommendations (for future use if connection emerges)
- **Lead with:** Christ-centered mission — the foundation's very name is the framing hook. "Waha's work is animated by the same conviction that gave your foundation its name: Christ IS our salvation — and we're helping ordinary believers share that salvation in 46+ languages."
- **Start-up framing:** Lean into Waha's newness as an organization (501(c)(3) established 2025) — CIOS's interest in start-up support is a real differentiator from foundations that only fund established organizations.
- **Education angle:** Frame the Disciple Making Course as community-based evangelical education — parallel to but distinct from formal seminary training.
- **Arts angle (if relevant to current board):** The Samford arts grant suggests openness to creative expressions of faith. Waha's audio production in indigenous languages could be framed as Scripture-as-cultural-expression.
- **Tone:** Warm, personal, Christ-centered. Baptist/evangelical. The Piper family responds to genuine faith conviction, not nonprofit professionalism.
- **Funder type:** Conservative evangelical / Baptist tradition. Full evangelical language appropriate.
