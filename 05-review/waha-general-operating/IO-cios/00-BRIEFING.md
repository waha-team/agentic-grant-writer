# Briefing: C.I.O.S. (Christ Is Our Salvation)

> **Status: IO — Invite Only / Family-Controlled, No Unsolicited Applications**
> C.I.O.S. is a family-controlled foundation established by Paul and Katy Piper in Waco, Texas. It does not accept unsolicited applications and has no website or public application process. Grants appear to flow primarily to Texas-based educational institutions (Baylor University, Samford University) and evangelical causes aligned with the Piper family's values. Despite large assets (~$110M), the foundation's giving has a local/institutional character that may not align with Waha's global digital missions model. Access requires personal connection to the Piper family.

*Research date: 2026-03-03*

## History & Context

Paul Piper Sr. was a Texas entrepreneur and evangelical Christian who founded Piper Industries in 1937 with $17 from his parents, growing it to a company of more than 1,000 employees ([Here-4-You Consulting / npfunds.com](https://npfunds.com/directory/c-i-o-s/)). He and his wife Katy established the CIOS Foundation in 1952 as an expression of their faith ([Northern Seminary](https://www.seminary.edu/news/christ-is-our-salvation-foundation-makes-large-grant-to-northern-benefiting-women-and-persons-of-color/)). The foundation continued after Paul Sr.'s death (Baylor held services for him ([Baylor University](https://www.baylor.edu/mediacommunications/news.php?action=story&story=6403)); the family has deep Baylor connections).

The book "Christ Is Our Salvation: A Life Story of Paul and Katy Piper" (by Lee Allen and Catherine Allen) documents the foundation's namesake and the Piper family's evangelical faith ([Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Christ-Our-Salvation-Story-Piper/dp/0962563420)).

Current leadership: Paul Piper Jr. (President) and his wife Shirley Piper (Program Officer); Katy Piper Jr. and Polly Piper Rickard are trustees ([Northern Seminary](https://www.seminary.edu/news/christ-is-our-salvation-foundation-makes-large-grant-to-northern-benefiting-women-and-persons-of-color/)).

The Baylor and Samford connections suggest alignment with Baptist-tradition evangelical Christianity — Southern Baptist or similarly conservative Protestant. The arts grant (Samford Center for Worship and the Arts) is interesting — suggests some openness to Christian arts and worship alongside evangelical missions.

With $110M in assets, this is a significant foundation — but with limited public transparency about how it gives.

## Why Waha Is a Fit

Theoretical alignment:
- "Christian evangelical purposes" — Waha is explicitly evangelical
- "Start-up operations and program development" — Waha received its 501(c)(3) in 2025 and is building organizational infrastructure; could frame as start-up phase support
- Arts and worship angle (Samford grant) — Waha's audio-produced, culturally adapted curriculum could be framed as worship in heart languages
- "Christ Is Our Salvation" theology — directly consistent with Waha's disciple-making focus

Practical access is very limited without a Texas/Baylor/Piper family connection.

## Why They Might Say No

- **No unsolicited applications:** Explicit policy
- **Local/institutional focus:** Confirmed grants to Texas universities suggest a local orientation that may not extend to a global digital missions platform
- **Baptist tradition:** If the foundation is closely aligned with Southern Baptist Convention institutions, Waha's non-denominational approach may be a lesser fit than SBC-affiliated organizations
- **Private and inaccessible:** No website, no public process, family-controlled
- **Size vs. access mismatch:** Large assets ($110M) but no public intake mechanism
