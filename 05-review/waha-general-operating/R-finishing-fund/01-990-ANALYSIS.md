- **2023 grants total:** Filed August 2024 — "26 awards in 2023" (exact total not confirmed in public summary) ([ProPublica 990, 2023](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/822745658))
- **Lifetime giving:** $5.4M+ to ministry partners since founding ([Cause IQ](https://www.causeiq.com/organizations/finishing-fund-management,822745658/))
- **Number of grants (2022):** 25 partners ([ProPublica 990, 2022](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/822745658))
- **Number of grants (2023):** 26 awards ([ProPublica 990, 2023](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/822745658))
- **Grant range (2022):** $5,871 – $824,385 ([ProPublica 990, 2022](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/822745658))
- **Average grant size (2022):** ~$97,335
- **Median grant size:** Estimated $50,000–$75,000 (wide spread pulls average up)
- **Most common grant size bracket:** $25,000 – $100,000
- **Annual giving trend:** Growing. Fund was newer in 2021-2022; 2023 appears to maintain 25-26 partners annually.

## First-Time vs. Repeat Grantee Analysis
- **Do they give to new organizations?** Yes — they add new mission partners each year (26 in 2023 vs. 25 in 2022 suggests incremental new partners)
- **Typical first-time grant amount:** Unknown — full grantee list with individual amounts requires 990-PF Schedule I download. Based on range, a first-time ask of $25,000–$50,000 is reasonable.
- **Do first-time grantees grow over time?** Pattern unknown without multi-year grantee data.
- **Core/repeat grantees:** Unknown without full Schedule I. However, GFM describes "40+ projects in 50+ countries" — suggesting significant established portfolio.

## Geographic Patterns
- **Where do their grantees operate?** 50+ countries, with specific focus on regions with unengaged unreached people groups — Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, Southeast Asia, the Middle East, Central Asia, and remote Pacific regions. ([Foundation Website](https://www.globalfrontiermissions.org/unreached-people-group-fund))
- Very focused on "closed countries" and regions hostile to Christianity — exactly where Waha's security features matter.

## Thematic Patterns
- **What types of work do they actually fund?**
  - Indigenous missionaries and evangelists deployed to UUPGs (~primary)
  - Cross-cultural workers in "closed countries"
  - Church planting among first-generation believers in UUPG contexts
  - Some support for enabling infrastructure (training, resources) for indigenous leaders
- **Any surprising grants?** The wide range ($5,871 to $824,385) suggests the fund both supports tiny field projects (individual missionaries) and larger organizational partners. This is a "big tent" fund, not narrowly defined.

## Similar Organizations Funded
The full grantee list is not publicly available without Schedule I download. However, based on GFM's stated focus:
- Organizations sending indigenous believers to UUPGs
- Cross-cultural mission agencies serving in restricted-access nations
- Church planting movements among specific unreached people groups

Waha's closest analog among their grantees would be organizations that provide resources or tools for indigenous church planters — not translation agencies, not Western sending organizations, but the enabling infrastructure for national believers to multiply.

## Recommended Ask Amount
- **First-time ask:** $25,000 – $50,000 — tied to a specific language launch that targets a UUPG (e.g., a specific language serving an unreached people group in the pipeline).
- **Future relationship ask:** $75,000 – $150,000 — if framed around Waha's full pipeline of UUPG-adjacent language launches.
- **Rationale:** The fund's 25-26 partners with a range of $5K–$824K suggests flexibility. A $25K-$50K first ask positions Waha as a field-infrastructure partner without overreaching. Frame around a specific UUPG language — not general operating support.
- **Important note:** This fund may not be the right vehicle for general operating support. Frame as "funding Waha's capacity to localize into X UUPG languages per year" rather than "fund our operations."
