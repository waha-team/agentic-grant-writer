## History & Context

The Finishing Fund was established as a fund vehicle for high-impact giving to the "Finishing the Task" movement — the evangelical coalition focused on ensuring every remaining unengaged unreached people group (UUPG) has at least one gospel witness. Finishing the Task estimates ~189 UUPGs with 5.7M+ people still with no evangelical witness.

The fund operates through Global Frontier Missions, which serves as the awareness and intake arm. GFM is a well-regarded short-term and career missions training organization (MinistryWatch rated). The fund itself (Finishing Fund Management Inc) is the 501(c)(3) entity.

The NCF (National Christian Foundation) connection: The fund appears to operate within or alongside the NCF ecosystem, which is the largest Christian DAF platform in the country. This connection broadens the donor base and provides infrastructure.

**CRM note:** Waha's CRM shows email interactions with The Finishing Fund beginning December 3, 2025, with the most recent interaction on February 2, 2026. The CRM description notes "GACX connection." This is an active, not cold, relationship. The exact nature of these interactions is not captured in notes, but the email history suggests Waha has already initiated contact.

## Why Waha Is a Fit

Waha's 46+ language platform, with 69 more in the pipeline, directly serves the Finishing the Task mission: equipping indigenous believers in unreached people groups to make disciples. Waha doesn't send foreign missionaries — it equips national believers in the unreached group's own heart language to lead Discovery Bible Studies. This is precisely what indigenous mission multiplication looks like at scale.

The specific framing for Finishing Fund: each language Waha localizes represents engagement with a specific people group. Waha's Amharic, Malagasy, Urdu, and other language launches each touch a specific population that the Finishing the Task movement cares about. The fund's focus on UUPGs aligns with Waha's pipeline of 69+ languages — many of which are minority languages spoken by unreached or underreached peoples.

Additionally, Waha's security features (disguise mode, offline operation) enable use in nations where overt missionary activity is impossible — exactly the "closed countries" context the Finishing Fund cares about.

## Why They Might Say No

- **Technology vs. people:** The Finishing Fund primarily funds the deployment of actual people — missionaries, evangelists, indigenous church planters. Waha is a technology platform. The fund may view technology as infrastructure, not a primary investment.
- **Unreached specificity:** The fund focuses very specifically on "unengaged, unreached people groups" — those with literally no gospel witness. Waha serves a broader population (including the churched, seekers, and under-reached communities). Waha needs to frame specifically around UUPGs in its pipeline.
- **Partnership model:** Without a GFM or network connection, there's no clear intake path.
- **Size calibration:** The fund's grants range widely. Waha would need to identify which component of its work maps most directly to UUPG engagement.
