### Who We Are

Waha is a Christian nonprofit building free, audio-based Discovery Bible Study tools for believers worldwide. Our mission is simple: empowering any person — regardless of literacy, location, or expertise — to facilitate a Scripture discovery conversation in their community. The format could not be simpler: gather, press play, discuss.

We are explicit about what we are: a Great Commission organization. We exist because Matthew 28:18-20 commands every believer to make disciples — and most believers have never been shown how. Waha closes that gap.

Our track record: 82,000+ equipped disciple-makers, 177+ countries with active users, 46+ languages available, 71,000+ Discovery Bible Studies facilitated — all through organic, word-of-mouth growth with near-zero marketing spend. Partners include Wycliffe Bible Translators, Cru, Pioneers, Frontiers, YWAM, YouVersion (500M+ installs), 24:14, and Biblica.

Waha is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit based in Spokane, Washington. We are an explicitly evangelical Christian organization. We affirm that Jesus Christ is fully divine, fully human, crucified for our sins, and bodily risen from the dead. This conviction is the foundation of everything we build.

### The New Ministry: Amharic Discovery Bible Study

**This is new ground.**

There are 340 million Amharic speakers worldwide — the official language of Ethiopia, the heart language of the Ethiopian Orthodox and Evangelical communities, and the lingua franca of the Ethiopian and Eritrean diaspora across the Middle East, Europe, and North America.

Until now, not one of those 340 million people has had access to an audio-guided Discovery Bible Study in Amharic. No simple tool for a small group of Amharic speakers to gather, press play, and discover Scripture together — in their own heart language.

This is a new ministry. We are not asking you to sustain an existing operation. We are asking you to help plant a flag in new ground.

**What this new ministry will look like:**

An Amharic-speaking believer — in Addis Ababa, or in Dubai, or in Minneapolis — opens the Waha app. They see "Amharic" in the language list. They download the Foundations curriculum — audio-guided Scripture stories leading from Creation through the Life of Jesus. They gather a few neighbors or coworkers, press play, and listen together. They pause for discussion. Each person is challenged to apply what they've heard. Before they leave, one of them volunteers to start their own group.

That is new ministry. That is plowing new ground for a greater harvest.

### Why This Matters Now

We are currently launching 10+ new languages in 2026, with 69 total in the pipeline. The Amharic DBS is one of the most strategic: Ethiopia is one of the fastest-growing evangelical communities on earth, and the Ethiopian diaspora — spread across nations with high concentrations of unreached Muslim-background people — carries enormous disciple-making potential.

The multiplication model works. In the Philippines, 30 groups became 90 in 50 days through this approach. In North Africa, one woman using Waha DBS sparked a multi-country house church movement. In a restricted-access nation, a man in a militant organization encountered Scripture through a Waha group and came to faith.

We know from Waha users in Ethiopia and the diaspora that the demand for Amharic tools is high. This grant delivers what they need.
