# 990 Analysis: E91 Foundation

*Source: E91 Foundation quarterly reports (e91foundation.org/impact), E91 Foundation website FAQ, About Us page*
*Note: 990-PF not located on ProPublica. EIN not confirmed. Analysis based on self-reported grant data from foundation's public quarterly reports.*
*Research date: 2026-03-03*

---

## Giving Overview

- **Total grants per year:** Approximately $280,000–$450,000 annually (estimated from quarterly reports; Q1 2025: $70,908; Q3 2024: $110,076; Q1 2024: $82,076) ([Foundation Website — Q1 2025 Update](https://e91foundation.org/portfolio-posts/2025-first-quarter-update/); [Q3 2024 Update](https://e91foundation.org/portfolio-posts/2024-third-quarter-update/); [Q1 2024 Update](https://e91foundation.org/portfolio-posts/2024-first-quarter-update/))
- **Number of grants per year:** Approximately 30–60 (15–20 per quarter)
- **Median grant size:** $5,000 (stated explicitly on website) ([Foundation Website — FAQ](https://e91foundation.org/contact-us/faq/))
- **Average grant size:** Approximately $5,000–$8,000
- **Grant range:** $1,000–$25,000 (from quarterly reports; $25,000 appears to be reserved for E91 Church's own programs; outside grantees typically $2,500–$8,788)
- **Most common grant size bracket:** $5,000–$8,788
- **Stated endowment/fund size:** "Fund exceeds $3 million" (from website) ([Foundation Website — Impact](https://e91foundation.org/impact/))
- **Total granted since 1994:** "More than $2 million" to "300+ evangelical nonprofit ministries" ([Foundation Website — Impact](https://e91foundation.org/impact/))

**Note on $8,788 grants:** This precise amount appears repeatedly across multiple grantees and quarters (Allendale Christian Camp, Polish Christian Ministries). It appears to be a standard second-tier grant amount — likely reflecting a specific fund category or calculation formula within the foundation's structure.

## First-Time vs. Repeat Grantee Analysis

- **Do they give to new organizations?** Yes — LOI process is explicitly open to new applicants. No invitation required. ([Foundation Website — FAQ](https://e91foundation.org/contact-us/faq/))
- **Typical first-time grant amount:** $5,000 (standard grant amount stated on website) ([Foundation Website — FAQ](https://e91foundation.org/contact-us/faq/))
- **Do first-time grantees grow over time?** Some do — Afterbeat/Capstone Legacy Foundation received $5,000 in Q1 2024 and again in Q3 2024. ([Foundation Website — Q1 2024 Update](https://e91foundation.org/portfolio-posts/2024-first-quarter-update/); [Q3 2024 Update](https://e91foundation.org/portfolio-posts/2024-third-quarter-update/)) Alexander Christian Foundation appears at $16,000–$20,000 across multiple quarters, suggesting relationship growth.
- **Core/repeat grantees (appear across multiple quarters):**
  - Allendale Christian Camp (multiple quarters at $8,194–$8,788) — recurring youth ministry partner
  - Alexander Christian Foundation (multiple quarters at $16,000–$20,000) — recurring, higher amounts suggest trusted relationship
  - Polish Christian Ministries (multiple quarters at $8,194–$8,788) — recurring international ministry partner
  - E91 Church itself (internal grants — appears to receive significant amounts quarterly)
  - Afterbeat (multiple quarters at $5,000) — recurring international evangelism partner

**Implication for Waha:** Start at $5,000. If the relationship develops, subsequent rounds could be $5,000–$8,788. The foundation does maintain multi-year relationships with some grantees, despite saying they prefer one-time grants.

## Geographic Patterns

- **Where do their grantees operate?** Primarily international (Netherlands, Kenya, Nepal, Poland, Scotland, India, Romania, Belize, Europe broadly)
- **Local (Indianapolis):** Alexander Christian Foundation, Allendale Christian Camp, Shepherd Community Center, Kairos Benevolence Fund
- **Split:** Approximately 50/50 local-to-Indianapolis and international ministries
- **Waha implication:** International scope is fully acceptable and normal. WA-based headquarters is not a barrier.

## Similar Organizations Funded

1. **Crescent Project — $5,000 (Q1 2025, Muslim outreach expansion in Europe):** Most similar to Waha's cross-cultural evangelism work. Crescent Project trains Christians to share their faith with Muslims — comparable to Waha's work equipping believers to reach seekers in any culture. **This grant proves E91 funds cross-cultural outreach to non-Christian communities.**

2. **Cru/Campus Crusade for Christ — $5,000 (Q3 2024, development of 18 Nepali ministry leaders):** Waha is a Cru partner. This grant confirms E91 funds Waha's partner organizations. The "development of 18 Nepali ministry leaders" framing — specific new initiative, specific geography, specific people count — is exactly the model Waha should replicate in its proposal.

3. **Afterbeat/Capstone Legacy Foundation — $5,000 (Q1 and Q3 2024):** School evangelism in the Netherlands. New initiative, international scope, recurring grantee. Shows E91 funds international evangelism technology/methods.

4. **International Disaster Emergency Service — $5,000 (Q1 and Q3 2024, new church plant in Kenya Rift Valley):** "New church plant" = new ministry. This is the framing Waha should use: a new language launch = new ministry reaching people who have never had access before.

5. **Covenant Connections — $5,000 (Q3 2024, publishing ministry resources for African/Pakistani leaders):** Creating ministry resources for leaders in specific language/cultural contexts — very similar to what Waha does. International scope, resource creation, unreached leaders.

## Recommended Ask Amount

- **First-time ask:** **$5,000** — stated explicitly as the standard grant amount; consistent with all first-time grantees in the database
- **Future relationship ask:** $5,000–$8,788 — if relationship is maintained across cycles
- **Rationale:** E91 is very consistent with $5,000 first-time grants. Do not ask for more. The goal here is a one-time grant for a specific new initiative. A successful $5,000 grant can lead to $8,788 follow-on grants in future cycles.
