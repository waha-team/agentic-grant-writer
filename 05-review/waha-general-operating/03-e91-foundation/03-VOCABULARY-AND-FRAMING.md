## Their Stated Values (verbatim from website/FAQ)

1. **Primary purpose:** "Assisting the making of disciples" (Matthew 28:18-20) ([Foundation Website — FAQ](https://e91foundation.org/contact-us/faq/))
2. **Secondary purpose:** "Meeting the physical needs of hurting people" (Matthew 25:35-40) ([Foundation Website — FAQ](https://e91foundation.org/contact-us/faq/))
3. **Ministry focus:** "New and expanding ministries in the field of evangelism, church planting, benevolence, medical care, education, and missions" ([Foundation Website — About](https://e91foundation.org/about-us/))
4. **Foundation ethos:** "Plowing new ground for a greater harvest for the Kingdom of God" ([Foundation Website — About](https://e91foundation.org/about-us/))
5. **Organizational character:** "Faith and community to create better impact on the ministries" donors care about ([Foundation Website](https://e91foundation.org/))
6. **Non-support:** Does NOT fund "operating expenses of existing ministries" ([Foundation Website — FAQ](https://e91foundation.org/contact-us/faq/))

## How We Map to Each Value

1. **"Assisting the making of disciples" (Matthew 28:18-20)** → This is Waha's entire mission. We don't need to translate — this IS our mission statement in different words. "Waha exists to make disciples who multiply disciples, fulfilling the mandate of Matthew 28 by equipping any believer to lead a Discovery Bible Study."

2. **"Meeting the physical needs of hurting people" (Matthew 25:35-40)** → Secondary alignment: Waha reaches vulnerable populations — refugees, persecuted believers, people in poverty. Document stories of transformation that include physical needs met (Mauritius family freed from poverty, refugee communities given belonging).

3. **"New and expanding ministries... evangelism, church planting, missions"** → Frame the Amharic DBS launch as NEW: a first-ever audio curriculum for 340 million Amharic-speaking people who have never had access to Scripture in this format. This is a new ministry.

4. **"Plowing new ground"** → Perfect language for a new language launch. "Waha is plowing new ground among Amharic speakers — 340 million people who have never had access to an audio-guided Discovery Bible Study in their heart language."

5. **Does NOT fund operating expenses** → Do NOT frame this as general support. Frame as: specific, new, deliverable-focused initiative. The Amharic DBS launch is a project with a start, a middle, and a clear completion (when audio is recorded, licensed, and deployed in the app).

## Framing Recommendations

- **Lead with:** The specific new initiative — Amharic DBS launch. "For the first time in history, 340 million Amharic speakers will be able to open an app and hear the stories of Jesus in their own language." This is NEW GROUND.

- **Evangelize the problem first:** E91 is an evangelical foundation. They know what "unreached" means. Use the language freely: "7,000 unreached people groups," "the Great Commission," "making disciples among people who have never heard."

- **Make it about multiplication:** E91's grantees are often creating new access, new leaders, new infrastructure. Frame Waha's model as inherently multiplicative — the Amharic DBS doesn't just reach one person, it reaches one person who leads a group who leads more groups.

- **Avoid:** "General operating support," "organizational capacity," "infrastructure." These sound like existing operations. Every word must signal NEW.

- **Avoid:** Lengthy technology explanations. E91 doesn't fund technology for its own sake. The technology is the delivery mechanism; the mission is discipleship and evangelism. Mention "app" only as context; emphasize the spiritual outcome.

- **Tone:** Evangelical-insider, warm, story-first, urgent. This is the funder for whom you can say "The Great Commission" without explanation. Lean into that shared language.

- **Funder type:** Explicitly evangelical — this is E91's identity. Use all evangelical vocabulary freely.

- **Story use:** Lead with a specific story from the target people group or closest analog — a Muslim background believer reached, a first-generation disciple who started a movement, a refugee finding hope. E91 responds to human stories of spiritual transformation.

## Specific Grants They've Made That Relate to Us

1. **Crescent Project — $5,000 (Q1 2025, Muslim outreach expansion in Europe):** Proves E91 funds evangelism to Muslim populations. Waha's Amharic launch reaches many Muslim-background Ethiopians and Eritreans. "Similar because: cross-cultural evangelism targeting people of Muslim background."

2. **Cru/Campus Crusade for Christ — $5,000 (Q3 2024, development of 18 Nepali ministry leaders):** Waha's partner organization. Proves E91 funds Waha's ecosystem. "Similar because: training new disciple-making leaders in a specific unreached context — identical to what Waha does with every language launch."

3. **Afterbeat/Capstone Legacy Foundation — $5,000 (Q1 and Q3 2024, school evangelism in the Netherlands):** Recurring grant to international evangelism initiative. "Similar because: new ministry in a specific international context, scaled through repeat relationship."

4. **International Disaster Emergency Service — $5,000 (Q1 and Q3 2024, new church plant in Kenya Rift Valley):** E91 funds new church plants in new geographic contexts. A new DBS language launch is analogous — it's the infrastructure that enables new church plants to emerge.

5. **Covenant Connections — $5,000 (Q3 2024, publishing ministry resources for African/Pakistani leaders):** Creating discipleship resources for specific cultural contexts — direct parallel to Waha's localization model.

## Draft "Why We Fit" Paragraph (in their language)

The E91 Foundation exists to plow new ground for a greater harvest in the Kingdom of God — and that is exactly what Waha is doing in Amharic. For the first time, 340 million Amharic-speaking people — spread across Ethiopia, Eritrea, and diaspora communities worldwide — will have access to audio-guided Discovery Bible Studies in their heart language. This is new ground. This is new ministry. Waha equips ordinary believers to make disciples who make more disciples, fulfilling the mandate of Matthew 28:18-20 in a community that has never had access to Scripture engagement tools like this before. Like Cru's Nepali leadership development and the Crescent Project's Muslim outreach expansion — both of which E91 has supported — Waha's Amharic launch represents a specific, deliverable, new ministry initiative with the potential to catalyze a multiplying church movement among an unreached people community.
