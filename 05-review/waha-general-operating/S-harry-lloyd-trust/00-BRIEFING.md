## History & Context

Harry J. Lloyd was a successful businessman who designated a percentage of annual business profits to support Christian organizations throughout his lifetime ([FCA Hall of Champions](https://hall.fca.org/1998/harry-j-lloyd/)). Upon his death, a charitable trust was established in 1997 to continue his giving spirit ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_J._Lloyd)).

Cheryl Cole has been the identified contact for the trust (phone and email publicly listed), suggesting professional administration. This is NOT a family-controlled foundation in the same way as CIOS or DeMoss — it is professionally administered, which makes it MORE accessible. ([The Grantsmanship Center](https://www.tgci.com/funder/harry-j-lloyd-charitable-trust))

The trust has funded over $35M in lifetime giving across evangelical missions and melanoma research. The dual focus (global missions + melanoma research) reflects Harry Lloyd's personal history (he died of melanoma) ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_J._Lloyd)).

**Key accessibility note:** The trust accepts written applications — a summary of no more than 3 pages with no formal deadline. This is one of the simplest application processes in our research set. IF the trust is actively grantmaking, Waha should pursue it directly.

- "Foreign evangelical missions" — Waha serves missionaries and national believers in 177+ countries
- "Africa, China, India, Latin America, Middle East" — Waha has active users in all these regions; Africa and the Middle East are among Waha's strongest growth areas
- "Global missions organizations based in the United States serving those areas" — Waha is based in Spokane, WA and serves globally

Specific language matches:
- "Spreading evangelism throughout the world" → Waha empowers ordinary believers to share Scripture in 46+ languages
- Foreign missions focus → Waha's international user base (177+ countries) and localization pipeline

## Why They Might Say No

- **Active status uncertain:** The $0 grant year and subsequent $100K (possibly very reduced) giving raises questions about the trust's long-term trajectory
- **Melanoma research:** Roughly half of lifetime giving may go to melanoma research — which is not relevant to Waha
- **Small trust potentially winding down:** Trusts established in 1997 can have defined time horizons
- **Conservative investment approach:** The trust may be cautious with newer organizations (Waha received 501(c)(3) in 2025)
- **No website:** Suggests the trust maintains a lower profile and may have reduced administrative capacity
