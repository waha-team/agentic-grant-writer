## The Problem

### Missions isn't scaling — and the bottleneck is method, not motivation

Since 1900, the percentage of the world's population that identifies as Christian has not grown. **Zero percent increase in 125 years** (Lausanne). Only 17% of churchgoers can identify and explain the Great Commission (Barna). The global church has more resources, more technology, and more mobility than any generation in history — and the needle hasn't moved.

The core issue is threefold:

1. **The training gap.** Most believers want to obey the Great Commission but have never been shown how. Disciple-making has been professionalized — delegated to pastors, missionaries, and seminary graduates. The 99% of believers who are "ordinary" sit on the sidelines.

2. **The last-mile gap.** Bible translation organizations have done extraordinary work — Scripture exists in thousands of languages. But translation alone doesn't produce transformation. There is no simple pathway from "translation completed" to "communities changed." Translated Bibles sit on shelves (physical and digital) without a mechanism to put them into practice through community-based discovery.

3. **The access gap.** Billions of people live in oral cultures where reading is not the primary mode of learning. Hundreds of millions live in restricted-access nations where open Christian activity is dangerous or illegal. Still more live without reliable internet or electricity. Traditional discipleship methods — books, classrooms, seminaries, Sunday services — cannot reach these populations.

These three gaps compound each other. The result: **7,000 unreached people groups** — communities with little to no access to the Gospel — and a global church that is growing by addition at best, while the Great Commission requires multiplication.

### What happens if nothing changes

Without a scalable, accessible, multiplication-focused discipleship infrastructure, the global church will continue to plateau. Translated Scriptures will remain underutilized. National believers in the Global South — the fastest-growing segment of the church — will lack the tools to become the disciple-makers their communities need. And the 7,000 unreached people groups will remain unreached.

## What Waha Does

Waha is a free, audio-based mobile app and training platform that empowers ordinary believers to lead Discovery Bible Studies and make disciples — no seminary degree, no ministry training, no special equipment required. The model is radically simple: **gather, press play, discuss.**

Waha operates under three program pillars:

### 1. Equip — Product ($312,000 / 26% of budget)
Build and maintain the Waha app and its supporting technology infrastructure. This includes app development across iOS, Android, and web; project management; and "Hidden-in-Plain-Sight" hardware units (SD card and solar-powered audio players for communities without smartphones or internet).

**Key capabilities:**
- Audio-first design serving oral learners and low-literacy populations
- Fully offline after initial download — critical for rural and infrastructure-poor areas
- Security mode (disguise behind calculator or piano interface, passcode-protected) for believers in persecuted regions
- Encrypted at rest and in transit, audited by CISSP/CISM/CEH certified consultant
- SD card and solar player compatible for people without smartphones
- Full accessibility: ASL video lessons, screen reader support

### 2. Empower — Mobilization ($456,000 / 32% of budget)
Inspire, train, and coach ordinary believers to become active disciple-makers. This is the largest program area because Waha's theory of change depends on **people**, not just technology. The app is the tool; mobilization is what puts it in people's hands.

**Key activities:**
- Cohort-based Disciple Making Course (DMC) training — a 9-week interactive course that teaches believers why and how to make disciples
- Regional coordination across Asia, East Asia, North America, and the Deaf community
- Travel for field worker training and partnership development
- Marketing (currently near-zero spend; all growth has been organic and product-led)
- Coaching for emerging disciple-making leaders worldwide

### 3. Everywhere — Localization ($312,000 / 26% of budget)
Translate and localize Waha's DBS curriculum and DMC into new languages so multiplication can happen in every language and culture. Currently live in 46+ languages with 69 more in the pipeline.

**Key activities:**
- Translation and cultural adaptation of curriculum by native speakers
- Audio production — every lesson recorded in-language
- Bible licensing (Waha uses ~7% of Bible verses, enabling launch before full translation is complete)
- App interface localization (menus, buttons, navigation)
- Testing and deployment with local users

### Mission Support ($192,000 / 16% of budget)
Bookkeeping, office space, administration, and fundraising — the infrastructure that keeps the other three pillars running.

## Who Waha Serves

- **Cross-cultural workers** serving in contexts where they don't speak the local language
- **National believers** in unreached and restricted-access regions who want to share their faith but lack resources in their language
- **Oral learners** — people whose primary learning mode is listening, a significant portion of the global population (estimated 70%)
- **Persecuted believers** in restricted-access nations who need discreet, secure discipleship tools
- **Everyday Christians** — youth pastors, families, laypeople — who want to obey the Great Commission but don't know how to start
- **Seekers** — non-believers encountering Scripture for the first time through Waha groups (86% of Waha's installs are from seekers)

**Geographic reach:** 177+ countries with active users. Strongest presence in Sub-Saharan Africa, the Middle East, South and Southeast Asia, with growing adoption in Europe and the Americas.

**Scale:** 82,000+ total installs, ~6,200 monthly active users, ~2,000 new users per month — all with near-zero marketing spend.

## Sustainability

Waha's model is inherently sustainable:

1. **Zero marginal cost per user.** Once a language is deployed, the content is available indefinitely at no additional cost. Every grant dollar produces a permanent resource.
2. **Multiplication, not addition.** The DMC trains disciple-makers who train more disciple-makers. Growth does not depend on Waha's staff size.
3. **Diversified revenue.** Waha is actively building a portfolio of foundation grants, major donors, and grassroots supporters — reducing dependency on any single source.
4. **Lean operations.** A team of ~12 serves 82,000+ users in 177+ countries. Technology scales efficiently.
5. **Proven organic growth.** 82,000 installs with near-zero marketing spend demonstrates product-market fit that does not depend on paid acquisition.

## Security Considerations

Waha operates in restricted-access nations where believers face persecution. Security features (disguise mode, encryption, offline capability) are built into the product. Some funders may prefer that proposals not name specific countries or individuals in sensitive regions. Proposals for this brief should use general language about "restricted-access nations" and anonymized testimonials unless the funder specifically operates in persecution-focused grantmaking.

## Funder Search Guidance

Because this brief targets **general operating support** rather than a single project, the funder search should prioritize:

1. **Foundations that explicitly offer general operating / unrestricted grants** to Christian nonprofits or missions organizations
2. **Large evangelical foundations** with a track record of funding missions infrastructure (not just individual projects)
3. **Foundations focused on Great Commission / church planting / disciple-making movements** at an organizational level
4. **Technology-for-ministry funders** who invest in platforms and tools rather than single programs
5. **Capacity-building and organizational development** funders in the faith-based space
6. **Foundations with $25,000+ typical grant sizes** — general operating grants tend to be larger than project grants

**Grant types to look for in 990 analysis:**
- "General support" or "operating support" line items
- Grants to organizations like Cru, YWAM, IMB, Pioneers, Frontiers (peer missions orgs)
- Multi-year grants (indicates willingness to invest in organizational sustainability)
- Grants described as "capacity building," "infrastructure," or "organizational development"

**Potential ask range:** $10,000 – $150,000 per foundation, sized to their typical grant patterns. Larger asks are appropriate for general operating grants than for single-language projects.
