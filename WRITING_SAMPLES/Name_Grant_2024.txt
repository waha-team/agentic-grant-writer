# 2024 NAME Grant Application


Prior Grant Follow-Up: How was the money spent? And what was the outcome of the project in terms of multigenerational disciple making (disciples, generations, and depth)?

* In last year’s application, we requested funding to launch 10 new NAME language projects to Waha. We are happy to report that we have successfully added 9 NAME languages, with the 10th language (Hassaniya Arabic) scheduled to launch by November 2024.
* We are also happy to report that Waha as a whole now has 33 total languages, including a very exciting release of our first sign language, American Sign Language.
* The money received from the previous grant went to:
  * Paying a portion of our Localization Director’s salary, resulting in a 353% increase speed for launching new languages (we went from launching a new language every 12 weeks to now every 2.6 weeks). 
  * Paying a portion of our Full Stack Software Engineer’s salary, who helped significantly in both our localization processes and optimizing the app. For example, he and our Localization Director created an AI-powered time stamping tool to save hundreds of hours in generating hundreds of audio Bible mp3s for our DBS curriculum.
  * Paying a portion of our Bible Licensing Administrator’s salary, who plays a crucial role in the laborious—and often frustrating—process of gaining permission to use Bible translations legally on the app
  * Paying a portion of our servers/hosting/digital services expenses
  * Paying contractors for translation, proofreading, creating timing data, video animation syncing, voice narration, audio engineering, etc
  * Paying a portion of the operational costs required to maintain and grow Waha
* We are happy to report the following in terms of multigenerational disciple making:
  * Reports of stage 3 and 4 movements starting in NAME and elsewhere globally (see section below for more details)
  * 506% increase in introductory DMM trainings using the Disciple Making Course in the NAME region (133 → 807 )
  * 63% increase in facilitators hosting groups in the NAME region (688 → 1,126 facilitators)
  * 140% increase in meetings completed in the NAME region (1,559 → 3,740 Discovery Bible Studies)



Organization History & Vision (three sentences or fewer):  

Waha is a ministry of Mosaic Community Church Seattle—part of the Antioch Movement of churches—and was started by a DMM-focused Mosaic church planting team in Northwest Africa. Our vision is to empower people everywhere to multiply disciples and churches among the unreached through our Discovery Bible Study app and Disciple Making Course.

Ministry Profile: Please describe your ministry within the country(ies) for which you are applying for this grant.

Waha is a Discovery Bible Study and DMM training tool that has been used to host meetings in every country across the NAME region, except for Mauritania (which will change once we launch Hassaniya Arabic in November and start mobilization for that language group). Waha makes obeying the Great Commission easy for minimally trained, ordinary, and even illiterate people, by providing a curated DBS curriculum that guides them to discover, obey, and share God’s Word with their communities.

Key Outcomes & Impact Expected: 
In one or two paragraphs, what are the major outcomes and intended impact of your project?  Please include:
  - How you expect this project to accelerate disciple making movements among Mu$1ims.

With the grant we received last year, we were able to add Levantine Arabic to Waha. “Steve”, an American missionary, did the Waha Disciple Making Course with a group of 8 local believers in Jordan. That group was able to immediately start one discovery group (using the newly added Levantine Arabic!) after the training. In the disciple maker’s words, they got “hooked” after that. Now they have started dozens of new groups, reaching consistent third generation. They are using the topical stories in Waha and then going through the Creation to Christ lessons.

Through simply reading scripture, applying it, and sharing it with others, we are seeing many case studies world wide (and in the NAME region) of groups getting catalyzed and multiplying. Over the past 12 months, globally, Waha has doubled in usage, reaching now over 33,000 Discovery Bible Studies facilitated! This number is likely much higher, as we are unable to track lessons that have been shared fully offline (like as a PDF or WhatsApp mp3).

For this grant, we need to increase the availability of DMM resources (Waha DBS and the Disciple Making Course) in low-resource languages. Every week we get new requests to add languages, so we prioritize adding the languages where there is an existing movement, network, or community to launch to. Our goal for this grant is to add 10 more NAME languages.

Additionally, with the same excellence we revamped out translation process (thanks to the grant you funded!), we want to build out a robust mobilization process. So this means, dramatically increasing networking, training, and coaching throughout both NAME and other regions. Part of that would be to build out a regional coordinator network. These regional coordinators would be largely self-funded, with a small stipend from our team and we would cover some travel expenses. Just as we hired a Localization Director with great success, we need to expand our team with a Mobilization Director to coordinate a team of 30 regional coordinators.


  - Thoughts on how your strategy promotes sustainable results that may reduce the need for future outside investment.

We model our financial strategies after YouVersion and the BibleProject. Half of their operating budgets come from small monthly “crowd funders” - we have kicked this process off in the last year with the roll out of a new website and we are continuing to develop that program. Additionally, Waha is a catalytic use of funds that does not create any type of economic dependence between Western money and NAME region locals.

Other Descriptions (OPTIONAL): 

“R” is a local pastor. He has a vision to reach not only his city, but his whole country, and the surrounding countries to the east. His vision is to equip, train, and commission Kingdom Laborers to plant churches. But he knows there isn’t enough funding available to pay salaries for these Kingdom Laborers. He sees that these people need to be bi-vocational ministers. He just isn’t sure how to do this both strategically or tactically. After learning about DMM at the 2024 Lausanne Congress, “R” realized that there is a sustainable approach to making disciples. He loved the emphasis on everyone having a shared responsibility for ministry, not just the paid staff. But he wasn’t sure how to get started with DMM. By chance, I (Jeff), was at this same conference. In R’s words, “Waha is a gift from God. This is exactly what I need to get started with DMM”. R is now training his leaders with the Waha Disciple Making Course while a member of our team coaches him.

We are finding that more and more people are adopting DMM as a strategy. However, they are struggling with the apostolic work of going from zero to one. They need help breaking off previous paradigms of ministry. Waha is increasingly getting pointed to as a resource to help people get started. This was our original vision when we first started! We asked ourselves, “how can we best mobilize, train, and hold accountable local believers to start making steps towards movement”? 

Additionally, according to the State of the Great Commission Report (Laussane, 2024), there are three areas that the global body is identifying as needs for finishing the Great Commission: Increased disciple making, disciple maturing, and tools for a digital age. We feel like Waha is hitting on all of three of these areas.

Budget & Schedule Detail: 

The cost to continue to improve Waha as a product, rapidly add new languages, market, train, and mobilize local disciple makers to use it averages about $50,000 per language. We were able to shave $10,000 off this number with improvements that last year’s grant helped fund! Compared to the average cost of translating the Bible into a new language ($1.2 million), this amount is leading to significant scripture engagement, disciple makers equipped, churches planted, and disciple making movements catalyzed. Our requested amount of $250,000 would cover half of the cost to launch the 10 NAME languages this year. 

In the coming 12 months, we will add the following NAME languages to Waha: Egyptian Sign Language DBS, Hebrew DBS / DMC, Sudanese Arabic DBS, Somali DBS, Azeri DBS, Southern Kurdish DBS, Armenian DBS, Georgian DBS, Pashto DBS, and Nafusi (Libyan Berber) DBS. Additionally, we will be able to hire 30 low cost regional coordinators and a mobilization director, so as to dramatically increase training, coaching, networking, and marketing among local believers in the NAME region and beyond.

While adding particularly low-resource languages (like Egyptian Sign Language) may not result in an immediate boost of overall Waha numbers, we continue to believe that creating DMM resources in these frontier people groups is essential for the completion of the Great Commission. We humbly and fervently request your continued partnership to help us make it happen.

Our target budget for Waha this year is $1,200,000. Our NAME activity accounts for roughly 40% of our efforts and resource allotment, which explains our $500,000 total project cost.
